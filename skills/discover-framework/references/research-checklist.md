# Framework Research Checklist

This checklist guides comprehensive research when investigating a framework for addition to the Strategic Frameworks library. Use this systematically to ensure thorough vetting.

---

## Research Process

### Step 1: Initial Validation (5 minutes)

Before deep research, quickly validate framework is worth investigating:

**Quick checks:**
- [ ] Framework has a clear name (not just a vague concept)
- [ ] You can find at least 3 sources mentioning it
- [ ] Sources are credible (not random blogs)
- [ ] Framework appears to be real (not AI-generated)
- [ ] Someone besides creator uses/teaches it

**If all checks pass:** Proceed to deep research
**If any fail:** Stop and find alternative framework

---

### Step 2: Framework Identity (10-15 minutes)

Establish basic facts about the framework.

#### Creator Information

**What to find:**
- [ ] Creator's full name(s)
- [ ] Creator's credentials (title, company, education)
- [ ] Creator's expertise area
- [ ] Why creator is credible source

**Research sources:**
- Framework's official website
- Creator's LinkedIn, Wikipedia, or professional bio
- Book author bios
- Academic papers (author information)

**Red flags:**
🚩 Creator can't be identified
🚩 Creator has no relevant expertise
🚩 Creator is unknown with no credentials
🚩 "Framework" attributed to AI or anonymous

**Good signs:**
✅ Creator is recognized expert in field
✅ Creator has published books/papers
✅ Creator has track record of impact
✅ Creator's credentials are relevant

---

#### Origin & History

**What to find:**
- [ ] Year framework was created/published
- [ ] Original publication (book, paper, talk)
- [ ] Historical context (why was it created?)
- [ ] Problem it was solving at the time
- [ ] How it evolved since creation

**Research sources:**
- Original book or paper
- Framework origin story on official sites
- Historical articles about framework
- Interviews with creator

**Red flags:**
🚩 No clear origin date
🚩 No original publication
🚩 Origin story is vague or changes
🚩 Created very recently with no validation

**Good signs:**
✅ Clear publication date and source
✅ Well-documented origin story
✅ Evolved over time with refinements
✅ Historical context makes sense

---

### Step 3: Framework Methodology (15-20 minutes)

Understand how the framework actually works.

#### Structure & Components

**What to find:**
- [ ] Framework phases/steps (how many?)
- [ ] Sequence and flow (linear, cyclical, flexible?)
- [ ] Key components or elements
- [ ] Core concepts or principles
- [ ] Unique methodology or approach

**Research sources:**
- Framework documentation
- Books and guides
- Official website or resources
- Tutorial videos or courses
- Academic papers describing it

**Questions to answer:**
- What are the specific steps?
- What's the input and output?
- How long does it typically take?
- What makes it different from similar frameworks?
- What's the theoretical foundation?

**Red flags:**
🚩 Structure is vague or unclear
🚩 No specific steps or process
🚩 Seems like repackaged common sense
🚩 Methodology is incoherent
🚩 "Framework" is just list of questions

**Good signs:**
✅ Clear, documented structure
✅ Specific phases with defined outcomes
✅ Coherent methodology
✅ Practical and actionable
✅ Theoretically grounded

---

#### Application Process

**What to find:**
- [ ] How to apply framework step-by-step
- [ ] What questions to ask in each phase
- [ ] What tools or templates exist
- [ ] Time commitment required
- [ ] Skills needed to apply it

**Research sources:**
- Implementation guides
- Workshops or courses teaching it
- Case studies showing application
- Practitioner accounts

**Questions to answer:**
- Can I explain how to use this?
- Are there clear facilitation questions?
- Do templates or tools exist?
- Is application documented somewhere?

**Red flags:**
🚩 No clear application process
🚩 Only creator can apply it properly
🚩 Requires extensive training/certification
🚩 Application is too vague to replicate

**Good signs:**
✅ Clear application steps
✅ Available tools/templates
✅ Can be learned and applied
✅ Well-documented process

---

### Step 4: Target Audience & Use Cases (10 minutes)

Understand who uses this framework and when.

#### Target Users

**What to find:**
- [ ] Primary user types (roles, titles)
- [ ] Skill level required
- [ ] Industries or domains
- [ ] Team vs individual application

**Research sources:**
- Framework documentation
- User testimonials
- Case studies
- Training programs (who attends?)

**Questions to answer:**
- Who is this framework for?
- What roles use it most?
- Is it for individuals, teams, or organizations?
- What expertise level is needed?

---

#### Use Cases

**What to find:**
- [ ] Problem types framework addresses
- [ ] Situations where it's most effective
- [ ] Situations where it's not appropriate
- [ ] Alternatives for different contexts

**Research sources:**
- Case studies
- Framework documentation
- Practitioner experiences
- Comparative analyses

**Questions to answer:**
- When should you use this framework?
- When shouldn't you use it?
- What problems does it solve best?
- What are the boundaries of applicability?

---

### Step 5: Real-World Validation (15-20 minutes)

Verify framework has real-world adoption and effectiveness.

#### Company Adoption

**What to find:**
- [ ] Specific companies using framework (with names)
- [ ] How companies use it (specific applications)
- [ ] Scale of adoption (team, company-wide, industry-wide)
- [ ] Duration of use (short experiment or ongoing practice)

**Research sources:**
- Case studies (most important!)
- Company blog posts
- Conference presentations
- Business publications
- Framework creator's client list

**Minimum standard:**
- At least 3 named companies/organizations
- Specific descriptions of usage (not just "Company X uses this")
- Mix of company sizes/types if possible

**Red flags:**
🚩 No specific company names
🚩 Only creator's company uses it
🚩 Companies mentioned are made-up or can't be verified
🚩 No evidence of actual usage beyond marketing

**Good signs:**
✅ Multiple real companies with names
✅ Specific use cases documented
✅ Mix of industries and company sizes
✅ Long-term adoption, not just experiments

---

#### Case Studies & Examples

**What to find:**
- [ ] At least 2-3 specific, detailed examples
- [ ] Situation/context for each example
- [ ] How framework was applied
- [ ] Specific outcomes or results
- [ ] Sources for examples (where documented?)

**Research sources:**
- Published case studies
- Business articles
- Books about framework
- Conference talks
- Academic papers

**Quality criteria for examples:**
- **Specific:** Names, dates, details (not "a company")
- **Complete:** Situation, application, outcome
- **Credible:** Documented somewhere verifiable
- **Relevant:** Demonstrates framework value

**Red flags:**
🚩 Examples are generic or hypothetical
🚩 No specific names or details
🚩 Outcomes are vague ("improved performance")
🚩 Examples can't be verified
🚩 Only 1 example exists

**Good signs:**
✅ 3+ specific, detailed examples
✅ Documented in credible sources
✅ Clear situation → application → outcome
✅ Measurable results when possible
✅ Diverse contexts (different industries/problems)

---

#### Thought Leaders & Practitioners

**What to find:**
- [ ] Recognized experts who use/teach framework
- [ ] Consultants specializing in framework
- [ ] Academics studying framework
- [ ] Authors writing about framework
- [ ] Community of practice

**Research sources:**
- Books and publications
- Conference speaker lists
- Consulting firms
- Academic papers
- LinkedIn (who lists this skill?)
- Training programs (who teaches it?)

**Questions to answer:**
- Who besides creator promotes this?
- Is there a community around it?
- Do credible experts endorse it?
- Is anyone studying it academically?

**Red flags:**
🚩 Only creator promotes it
🚩 No community or practitioners
🚩 No academic interest
🚩 Consultants selling it but not using it

**Good signs:**
✅ Multiple practitioners and teachers
✅ Community of users
✅ Academic validation
✅ Experts independently endorsing it

---

### Step 6: Source Quality & Availability (10 minutes)

Assess quality and accessibility of learning resources.

#### Source Credibility

**What to verify:**
- [ ] Original book or paper exists (with ISBN/DOI)
- [ ] Sources are from credible publishers/journals
- [ ] Multiple independent sources exist
- [ ] Sources are not all marketing material
- [ ] Academic validation (if applicable)

**Source hierarchy (best to worst):**
1. **Peer-reviewed academic papers** - Highest credibility
2. **Books from established publishers** - Very credible
3. **Industry publications** (HBR, McKinsey Quarterly) - Credible
4. **Professional blogs/sites** (thoughtful analysis) - Moderately credible
5. **General blogs** (varies widely) - Lower credibility
6. **Marketing material** (creator's promotional content) - Lowest credibility

**Minimum standard:**
- At least 3 credible sources
- At least one substantial source (book, academic paper, or major publication)
- Sources should be independent (not all from creator)

**Red flags:**
🚩 Only marketing material available
🚩 Sources are low-quality blogs
🚩 All sources are from creator/their company
🚩 Can't find original publication
🚩 Sources contradict each other

**Good signs:**
✅ Multiple high-quality sources
✅ Original book or paper available
✅ Independent validation
✅ Consistent information across sources
✅ Academic recognition

---

#### Learning Resources

**What to find:**
- [ ] Original book or paper
- [ ] Comprehensive guides or manuals
- [ ] Online courses or workshops
- [ ] Video explanations
- [ ] Official website with documentation
- [ ] Tools, templates, worksheets

**Research sources:**
- Amazon, Google Books (for books)
- Google Scholar (for papers)
- Coursera, Udemy, MasterClass (for courses)
- YouTube (for videos)
- Framework official sites

**Questions to answer:**
- Can someone learn this framework?
- Are learning resources accessible?
- Do resources cost money? (note for users)
- Are resources comprehensive or superficial?

**Good to have:**
✅ Free, accessible documentation
✅ Comprehensive learning materials
✅ Multiple formats (book, video, course)
✅ Practical tools and templates

---

### Step 7: Uniqueness Assessment (10 minutes)

Verify framework adds unique value to the library.

#### Comparison to Existing Frameworks

**What to check:**
- [ ] Review frameworks-index.md for similar frameworks
- [ ] Identify key differences from similar frameworks
- [ ] Determine unique value proposition
- [ ] Assess whether overlap is substantial or minor

**Questions to answer:**
- Does this framework duplicate an existing one?
- What does this framework offer that others don't?
- Is the difference meaningful or cosmetic?
- Would users benefit from having both?

**Red flags:**
🚩 Essentially same as existing framework
🚩 Only difference is name/branding
🚩 No unique value proposition
🚩 Would confuse users with duplicate

**Good signs:**
✅ Meaningfully different from existing frameworks
✅ Addresses gap in current library
✅ Offers unique perspective or methodology
✅ Complements rather than duplicates

---

#### Framework Positioning

**What to determine:**
- [ ] Category: Strategy / Decision / Innovation / Operations / Mental Models
- [ ] Position in framework ecosystem
- [ ] Complementary frameworks
- [ ] Alternative frameworks

**Questions to answer:**
- Where does this fit in our taxonomy?
- What frameworks does it compete with?
- What frameworks does it complement?
- When would you use this vs alternatives?

---

### Step 8: Practical Applicability (5 minutes)

Verify framework is actually usable.

#### Workshop Feasibility

**What to assess:**
- [ ] Can this be applied in facilitated session?
- [ ] Are there clear questions to ask?
- [ ] Is timeframe reasonable (30-90 min)?
- [ ] Do outputs make sense?
- [ ] Can you create workshop template?

**Questions to answer:**
- Can I facilitate this framework?
- Will users get value from it?
- Is it practical or just theoretical?
- Can it be documented in our format?

**Red flags:**
🚩 Too complex to apply in workshop
🚩 Requires specialized expertise
🚩 Purely theoretical with no practical steps
🚩 Can't be adapted to our format

**Good signs:**
✅ Clear application process
✅ Can be facilitated
✅ Reasonable time commitment
✅ Produces actionable outputs

## Research Tools

### WebSearch Queries

**Basic queries:**
- "[Framework name] creator"
- "[Framework name] origin history"
- "[Framework name] methodology"
- "[Framework name] case study"
- "[Framework name] examples"
- "[Framework name] book"
- "[Framework name] companies using"

**Validation queries:**
- "Who uses [framework name]"
- "[Framework name] success stories"
- "[Framework name] implementation"
- "How to apply [framework name]"
- "[Framework name] vs [similar framework]"

**Source quality queries:**
- "[Framework name] academic paper"
- "[Framework name] harvard business review"
- "[Framework name] mckinsey"
- "[Framework name] research"

### Source Evaluation

When evaluating sources, consider:

**High-quality indicators:**
- Peer-reviewed journals
- Major publishers (HBR Press, Wiley, etc.)
- Academic institutions
- Established consulting firms
- Original creator's published work

**Low-quality indicators:**
- Anonymous blogs
- Marketing material only
- Inconsistent information
- No citations or sources
- Recently created content with no validation

## Decision Criteria

After research, make decision:

### Add to Library If:
✅ Passes all quality criteria (see quality-criteria.md)
✅ Real framework with credible creator
✅ Clear methodology with structure
✅ Real-world validation (examples, users)
✅ Quality sources available (3+)
✅ Unique value vs existing frameworks
✅ Practically applicable
✅ Fits our framework taxonomy

### Don't Add If:
❌ Fails quality criteria
❌ Can't verify creator or sources
❌ No clear methodology
❌ No real-world validation
❌ Only marketing material exists
❌ Duplicates existing framework
❌ Too complex or theoretical to apply
❌ Outside our taxonomy scope

### Need More Research If:
⚠️ Some information missing
⚠️ Sources are borderline quality
⚠️ Unsure about uniqueness
⚠️ Applicability unclear

---

## Remember

**Thoroughness over speed:**
- Take time to research properly
- Don't skip sections
- Quality standards protect library

**Skepticism is healthy:**
- Many "frameworks" online are low-quality
- Verify everything
- Multiple credible sources required

**User perspective:**
- Will users find this useful?
- Can they actually apply it?
- Does it fill a gap?

**Library integrity:**
- Every framework must meet standards
- Better to reject marginal frameworks
- Quality over quantity
