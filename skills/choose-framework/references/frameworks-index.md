# Frameworks Index - Quick Reference

## Mental Models & Thinking (7 frameworks)

### 1. Mental Models - Latticework (Munger)
- **For whom**: Investors, business leaders, strategists, all complex decision-makers
- **Use case**: Multidimensional problem analysis, avoiding cognitive biases, investment decisions
- **Keywords**: interdisciplinary thinking, holistic approach, competitive advantage, deep understanding

### 3. First Principles Thinking (Musk)
- **For whom**: Innovators, entrepreneurs, engineers, problem solvers
- **Use case**: Breaking problems down to fundamentals, questioning assumptions, breakthrough solutions
- **Keywords**: innovation, fundamentals, assumptions, breakthrough, reduction to basics

### 5. Second-Order Thinking
- **For whom**: Strategists, leaders, long-term investors
- **Use case**: Thinking about consequences of consequences, avoiding short-term thinking traps
- **Keywords**: long-term consequences, second-order effects, strategic thinking

### 6. Inversion Thinking
- **For whom**: Strategists, risk managers, problem solvers
- **Use case**: Solving problems backwards, identifying pitfalls, risk management
- **Keywords**: inversion, avoiding failure, backward thinking, risk management

### 12. Six Thinking Hats (de Bono)
- **For whom**: Teams, facilitators, project leaders
- **Use case**: Structured thinking from different perspectives, avoiding conflicts in discussions
- **Keywords**: perspectives, structured discussion, team thinking, creativity

### 19. Circle of Competence (Buffett)
- **For whom**: Investors, decision-makers, all making decisions
- **Use case**: Knowing limits of own knowledge, focus on areas of expertise
- **Keywords**: knowledge boundaries, focus, specialization, self-awareness

### 48. SEEDS Model
- **For whom**: Decision-makers, leaders, all aware of cognitive biases
- **Use case**: Understanding and managing 5 categories of cognitive biases
- **Keywords**: cognitive biases, thinking errors, conscious decisions, debiasing

## Decision Making Frameworks (7 frameworks)

### 2. OODA Loop (Boyd)
- **For whom**: Leaders in dynamic environments, product managers, military, business
- **Use case**: Fast decision-making in changing environments, competitive advantage through speed
- **Keywords**: decision speed, adaptation, dynamic environment, competition

### 4. Cynefin Framework (Snowden)
- **For whom**: Leaders, managers, strategic consultants
- **Use case**: Managing in complexity, choosing right approach to problems
- **Keywords**: complexity, chaos, decision context, adaptive management

### 11. Eisenhower Matrix
- **For whom**: All needing better prioritization
- **Use case**: Time management, task prioritization, productivity
- **Keywords**: prioritization, productivity, important vs urgent, time management

### 18. Pre-Mortem Analysis (Klein)
- **For whom**: Project managers, strategists, project teams
- **Use case**: Risk identification before starting, better planning
- **Keywords**: risk identification, planning, failure, problem anticipation

### 21. Regret Minimization Framework (Bezos)
- **For whom**: Entrepreneurs, people facing life decisions, career changers
- **Use case**: Life decisions, career moves, long-term perspective
- **Keywords**: life decisions, long-term perspective, courage, regret, career change

### 42. Principled Negotiation (Fisher & Ury)
- **For whom**: Negotiators, leaders, all negotiating agreements
- **Use case**: Win-win negotiations, separating people from problems, focus on interests
- **Keywords**: negotiation, Getting to Yes, Harvard, interests vs positions

### 43. BATNA Framework
- **For whom**: Negotiators, deal makers, strategists
- **Use case**: Determining negotiation power through best alternative
- **Keywords**: Best Alternative, negotiation power, leverage, walk-away point

## Business Strategy & Models (12 frameworks)

### 8. Blue Ocean Strategy (Kim & Mauborgne)
- **For whom**: Entrepreneurs, strategists, business innovators
- **Use case**: Creating new market space, avoiding competition
- **Keywords**: new market, innovation, uncontested space, value

### 9. Porter's Five Forces
- **For whom**: Strategists, business analysts, entrepreneurs
- **Use case**: Industry competitive structure analysis, market entry strategy
- **Keywords**: competition, industry, market forces, structural analysis

### 17. Wardley Mapping (Wardley)
- **For whom**: Tech strategists, CTOs, product leaders
- **Use case**: Strategic mapping of component evolution, build vs buy decisions
- **Keywords**: technology evolution, strategic mapping, tech strategy

### 22. BCG Matrix
- **For whom**: Portfolio managers, corporate strategists, CFOs
- **Use case**: Product portfolio management, resource allocation
- **Keywords**: portfolio, resource allocation, growth, cash flow

### 23. Ansoff Matrix
- **For whom**: Growth strategists, product managers, CEOs
- **Use case**: Product-market growth strategies
- **Keywords**: growth, expansion, new markets, new products

### 24. McKinsey 7S Framework
- **For whom**: Change managers, consultants, transformation leaders
- **Use case**: Organizational alignment, transformations, M&A
- **Keywords**: organization, alignment, transformation, culture

### 25. Scenario Planning
- **For whom**: Long-term strategists, risk managers, leaders
- **Use case**: Planning in uncertainty, preparing for different futures
- **Keywords**: uncertainty, long-term planning, scenarios

### 29. Business Model Canvas (Osterwalder)
- **For whom**: Entrepreneurs, strategists, business consultants
- **Use case**: Visual mapping of 9 business model elements
- **Keywords**: business model, value proposition, customer segments, canvas

### 30. Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder)
- **For whom**: Product managers, entrepreneurs, marketers
- **Use case**: Product-market fit, matching value proposition to customer needs
- **Keywords**: customer jobs, pains, gains, product-market fit

### 31. Lean Canvas (Maurya)
- **For whom**: Startup founders, early-stage entrepreneurs
- **Use case**: Fast startup idea validation, focus on problem and solution
- **Keywords**: startup, MVP, problem-solution fit, unfair advantage

### 40. STP Model (Segmentation-Targeting-Positioning)
- **For whom**: Marketers, strategists, product marketers
- **Use case**: Market segmentation, target audience selection, positioning
- **Keywords**: segmentation, targeting, positioning, marketing strategy

### 47. Value Chain Analysis (Porter)
- **For whom**: Strategists, operations managers, consultants
- **Use case**: Analysis of primary and support activities for competitive advantage
- **Keywords**: value creation, primary activities, support activities, margin

## Product & Innovation (10 frameworks)

### 7. Jobs-to-be-Done (Christensen)
- **For whom**: Product managers, entrepreneurs, designers
- **Use case**: Understanding true customer needs, product innovation
- **Keywords**: customer needs, product-market fit, innovation

### 14. Design Thinking (IDEO/Stanford)
- **For whom**: Product managers, designers, innovators, creative teams
- **Use case**: Human-centered innovation, prototyping, rapid testing
- **Keywords**: empathy, prototyping, user-centric, iteration, innovation

### 15. Lean Startup (Ries)
- **For whom**: Startups, entrepreneurs, product teams
- **Use case**: Build-Measure-Learn in uncertainty, MVP
- **Keywords**: MVP, pivot, validated learning, iteration, startup

### 26. RICE Framework
- **For whom**: Product managers, teams prioritizing features
- **Use case**: Product prioritization (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort)
- **Keywords**: product prioritization, features, roadmap, data-driven

### 32. Kano Model (Noriaki Kano)
- **For whom**: Product managers, UX designers, customer success teams
- **Use case**: Feature classification by impact on customer satisfaction
- **Keywords**: Must-be, Performance, Delighters, customer satisfaction, feature prioritization

### 33. ICE Score (Sean Ellis)
- **For whom**: Growth teams, product managers, startup founders
- **Use case**: Quick experiment prioritization (Impact-Confidence-Ease)
- **Keywords**: growth hacking, prioritization, experiments, quick wins

### 34. MoSCoW Method (Dai Clegg)
- **For whom**: Project managers, product owners, agile teams
- **Use case**: Requirements categorization (Must-Should-Could-Won't)
- **Keywords**: requirements prioritization, scope management, agile, releases

### 35. Value vs Effort Matrix
- **For whom**: Product managers, project managers, leadership teams
- **Use case**: Visual 2×2 prioritization: Quick Wins, Major Projects, Fill-Ins, Time Sinks
- **Keywords**: impact vs effort, quick wins, prioritization matrix

### 39. AARRR Pirate Metrics (Dave McClure)
- **For whom**: Growth teams, startup founders, product managers
- **Use case**: Growth funnel: Acquisition-Activation-Retention-Revenue-Referral
- **Keywords**: growth hacking, pirate metrics, funnel optimization, startup metrics

### 41. RACE Framework (Dave Chaffey)
- **For whom**: Digital marketers, growth teams, marketing managers
- **Use case**: Digital marketing customer journey: Reach-Act-Convert-Engage
- **Keywords**: digital marketing, customer journey, lifecycle marketing

## Operations & Execution (4 frameworks)

### 10. Pareto Principle (80/20)
- **For whom**: All optimizing results
- **Use case**: Focus on highest-impact activities
- **Keywords**: prioritization, efficiency, focus, 80/20, leverage

### 13. Theory of Constraints (Goldratt)
- **For whom**: Operations managers, process optimizers, manufacturers
- **Use case**: Bottleneck identification and management, process optimization
- **Keywords**: bottleneck, optimization, throughput, processes

### 16. OKR Framework
- **For whom**: Leadership teams, product teams, all needing alignment
- **Use case**: Goal alignment, execution tracking, focus
- **Keywords**: objectives, key results, alignment, execution, tracking

## Sales Methodologies (3 frameworks)

### 36. SPIN Selling (Neil Rackham)
- **For whom**: B2B sales reps, account executives, sales managers
- **Use case**: Complex B2B sales through questions: Situation-Problem-Implication-Need-payoff
- **Keywords**: consultative selling, questioning framework, B2B sales, needs discovery

### 37. Challenger Sale (Dixon & Adamson)
- **For whom**: Enterprise sales, consultative sellers, sales leaders
- **Use case**: Teach-Tailor-Take Control, challenging customer assumptions
- **Keywords**: teaching, commercial insight, taking control, enterprise sales

### 38. MEDDIC/MEDDPICC
- **For whom**: Enterprise sales teams, B2B account executives
- **Use case**: Enterprise sales qualification: Metrics-Economic Buyer-Decision Criteria-Decision Process-Identify Pain-Champion
- **Keywords**: sales qualification, enterprise deals, complex sales, forecast accuracy

## Change Management (3 frameworks)

### 44. Kotter's 8-Step Change Model (John Kotter)
- **For whom**: Change leaders, executives, transformation managers
- **Use case**: 8 steps for large-scale organizational transformation
- **Keywords**: change management, transformation, urgency, vision, coalition

### 45. ADKAR Model (Prosci)
- **For whom**: Change managers, HR, team leaders
- **Use case**: Individual change: Awareness-Desire-Knowledge-Ability-Reinforcement
- **Keywords**: individual change, adoption, people-focused change, change readiness

### 46. Lewin's Change Model (Kurt Lewin)
- **For whom**: Change managers, team leaders, consultants
- **Use case**: Three-stage change: Unfreeze-Change-Refreeze
- **Keywords**: foundational change, three stages, organizational development

## Analysis & Planning (3 frameworks)

### 20. SWOT Analysis
- **For whom**: Strategists, planners, all analyzing situations
- **Use case**: Analysis of strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats
- **Keywords**: strategic analysis, strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats

### 27. Systems Thinking
- **For whom**: Strategists, problem solvers, policy makers
- **Use case**: Holistic approach to complex systems, understanding feedback loops
- **Keywords**: systems, holistic, feedback loops, complexity, interactions

### 28. Risk Management Frameworks
- **For whom**: Risk managers, compliance officers, project managers
