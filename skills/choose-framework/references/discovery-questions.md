# Discovery Questions Library

This library contains questions for exploring user situations during framework selection. Use these to understand context deeply before recommending frameworks.

**Usage:** Ask questions ONE AT A TIME, not as a list. Follow interesting threads. Adapt based on user responses.

---

## Problem Understanding Questions

Use these to clarify what the user is really trying to accomplish.

### Problem Type Clarification

**Strategic vs Operational:**
- "Are you trying to decide where to go, or how to get there?"
- "Is this about choosing direction or improving execution?"
- "Are you questioning what to do, or how to do it better?"

**Innovation vs Optimization:**
- "Are you creating something new, or improving something existing?"
- "Do you need to discover what users need, or optimize what you already know?"
- "Is this about finding a new way, or doing the current way better?"

**Decision vs Analysis:**
- "Are you trying to make a choice, or understand a situation?"
- "Do you need to decide between options, or figure out your options?"
- "Is the question 'what should I do' or 'what's happening here'?"

### Problem Depth

**Core problem:**
- "What problem are you really trying to solve?"
- "Why is this important right now?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this?"
- "What's the cost of inaction?"

**Problem clarity:**
- "How clearly can you describe the problem in one sentence?"
- "Is the problem clear or are you still figuring it out?"
- "What's uncertain about this situation?"

**Problem scope:**
- "Is this a big strategic problem or a tactical issue?"
- "How much of the organization/life does this affect?"
- "Is this about a single decision or a broader challenge?"

## Context Probing Questions

Use these to understand the environment and situation dynamics.

### Stability Assessment

**Predictability:**
- "How much is changing in your situation right now?"
- "Can you predict what will happen, or is it unpredictable?"
- "Is the environment stable or volatile?"
- "How often do the rules of the game change?"

**Cause-Effect Clarity:**
- "Do you know what causes what in this situation?"
- "Are the relationships clear or fuzzy?"
- "Can you point to clear cause-effect, or is it complex?"

**Uncertainty Level:**
- "What don't you know that you wish you knew?"
- "How many unknowns are there?"
- "Rate uncertainty: low, medium, or high?"
- "What assumptions are you making?"

### Competitive/Market Dynamics

**Competition:**
- "Who else is trying to solve this or compete in this space?"
- "How competitive is the environment?"
- "Is the market established or emerging?"
- "What's the pace of change in your industry/domain?"

**External Forces:**
- "What external factors affect this situation?"
- "What trends or forces are shaping this?"
- "What's outside your control that matters?"

---

## Time Horizon Questions

Use these to understand urgency and timeframe considerations.

### Urgency Assessment

**Immediate needs:**
- "When do you need to decide/act?"
- "What's forcing the timeline?"
- "Is this urgent, or can it wait?"
- "What's the consequence of delay?"

**Duration:**
- "How long will this take to implement?"
- "Are we talking days, months, or years?"
- "When do you need results?"

### Long-term Considerations

**Future impact:**
- "How far out are you thinking?"
- "Is this about next quarter or next decade?"
- "What timeframe are you optimizing for?"
- "Will this matter in 5 years? 10 years?"

**Reversibility:**
- "How easy is it to reverse this decision?"
- "Is this one-way or two-way door decision?" (Bezos framework)
- "Can you change course later, or is this permanent?"

---

## Stakeholder Questions

Use these to understand who's involved and affected.

### Decision Maker(s)

**Authority:**
- "Who's making the final decision?"
- "Is this your decision alone?"
- "Do you need buy-in from others?"
- "Who can veto this?"

**Role clarity:**
- "What's your role in this situation?"
- "Are you the founder, manager, employee, consultant?"
- "How much authority do you have?"

### Affected Parties

**Impact scope:**
- "Who's affected by this decision/problem?"
- "Just you, your team, the whole organization?"
- "Who has a stake in the outcome?"
- "Who cares about this besides you?"

**Implementation dependencies:**
- "Who needs to execute this?"
- "Whose cooperation do you need?"
- "Who can block implementation?"

### Perspectives

**Multiple viewpoints:**
- "How many different perspectives matter here?"
- "Do you need to consider conflicting stakeholder interests?"
- "Is there political complexity?"
- "Who might oppose this?"

---

## Data Availability Questions

Use these to understand what's known vs unknown.

### Existing Information

**Data richness:**
- "What data or information do you have?"
- "Have you done research on this?"
- "What's based on facts vs assumptions?"
- "Can you quantify this, or is it qualitative?"

**Validation:**
- "How do you know this is true?"
- "Have you validated these assumptions?"
- "Have you talked to users/customers/stakeholders?"
- "What evidence supports your thinking?"

### Unknowns

**Information gaps:**
- "What don't you know yet?"
- "What would you need to find out?"
- "Where are the biggest unknowns?"
- "What assumptions are you making?"

**Research feasibility:**
- "Can you get more data?"
- "Is it possible to validate assumptions?"
- "How long would research take?"
- "Do you need to decide now, or can you learn more?"

---

## Implementation Complexity Questions

Use these to understand execution requirements.

### Resource Requirements

**People:**
- "Can you do this alone, or do you need a team?"
- "How many people need to be involved?"
- "Do you have the right people?"
- "What skills are needed?"

**Time:**
- "How long will implementation take?"
- "What's your capacity to work on this?"
- "Is this a full-time effort or side project?"

**Budget/Resources:**
- "What resources do you need?"
- "What's the budget/cost?"
- "Do you have what you need, or need to acquire it?"

### Organizational Factors

**Change magnitude:**
- "How much organizational change is required?"
- "Is this a small tweak or major transformation?"
- "What needs to change in processes/systems/culture?"

**Resistance:**
- "What resistance might you face?"
- "Who might resist this?"
- "What obstacles exist?"
- "What's the risk of failure?"

## Framework Matching Questions

Use these to test framework fit explicitly.

### Experience & Familiarity

**Prior knowledge:**
- "Have you used frameworks before?"
- "Are you familiar with [specific frameworks]?"
- "What's your comfort level with structured approaches?"
- "Do you prefer analytical or creative frameworks?"

### Learning Style

**Preference:**
- "Do you prefer step-by-step processes or flexible approaches?"
- "Do you like structure or improvisation?"
- "Are you more analytical or intuitive?"

### Output Needs

**Desired outcomes:**
- "What do you want to get out of this?"
- "What does success look like?"
- "Do you need a decision, insights, a plan, or clarity?"
- "How will you use the framework output?"

### Constraints

**Practical limitations:**
- "How much time can you dedicate to this?"
- "Is a 30-minute or 3-hour framework better?"
- "Do you need something simple or comprehensive?"
- "Any constraints I should know about?"

## Follow-up & Probing Questions

Use these to dig deeper when answers are vague or interesting.

### Specificity Probes

**Get concrete:**
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "What does that look like in practice?"
- "Can you be more specific about [vague statement]?"
- "Walk me through exactly what that means"

### Assumption Challenges

**Test thinking:**
- "What are you assuming there?"
- "How do you know that's true?"
- "What if that assumption is wrong?"
- "What evidence supports that?"

### Consequence Exploration

**Second-order thinking:**
- "Then what happens?"
- "What's the consequence of that consequence?"
- "How does this affect other things?"
- "What ripple effects might occur?"

### Edge Case Testing

**Boundary conditions:**
- "What's the worst case scenario?"
- "What's the best case?"
- "What would make this fail?"
- "What happens if [extreme condition]?"

## Question Sequencing Patterns

### Pattern 1: Funnel (Broad → Specific)
1. "Tell me about your situation" (open)
2. "What type of problem is this?" (categorize)
3. "Give me a specific example" (concrete)

### Pattern 2: Ladder (Up Abstraction Levels)
1. "What specifically are you trying to do?" (concrete)
2. "Why does that matter?" (purpose)
3. "What's the bigger goal?" (strategic)

### Pattern 3: TEDW (Tell, Explain, Describe, Walk Through)
1. "Tell me about [X]"
2. "Explain how [X] works"
3. "Describe what happens when [condition]"
4. "Walk me through the process step-by-step"

### Pattern 4: Multi-Perspective
1. "From your perspective, what's the problem?"
2. "How would [other stakeholder] see this?"
3. "What would an outsider notice?"

## Questioning Best Practices

### Do:
✅ Ask one question at a time
✅ Wait for full answer before next question
✅ Follow interesting threads
✅ Adapt questions to user's answers
✅ Use user's language in follow-ups
✅ Circle back to unclear areas
✅ Probe vague answers
✅ Ask "why" to understand motivation

### Don't:
❌ Bombard with list of 10 questions
❌ Stick rigidly to script
❌ Accept superficial answers
❌ Move on when something's unclear
❌ Use jargon user doesn't understand
❌ Ask questions you already know answers to
❌ Make it feel like interrogation
❌ Rush through discovery

## Conversation Openers by Context

### When user mentions specific problem:
- "Tell me more about [problem they mentioned]"
- "What led to this [problem]?"
- "How long has this been an issue?"

### When user is vague:
- "Help me understand what you're trying to accomplish"
- "What's the situation you're dealing with?"
- "Where are you and where do you want to be?"

### When user has some clarity:
- "You mentioned [X]. How does that connect to [Y]?"
- "Walk me through what you've figured out so far"
- "What have you tried already?"

### When user is stuck:
- "What's the hardest part of this?"
- "Where are you feeling blocked?"
- "What would make this clearer?"

---

## Remember

**Quality > Quantity:** 5 great questions beat 20 surface questions

**Listen > Ask:** Pay attention to answers, don't just wait to ask next question

**Follow > Script:** Let conversation flow naturally, don't force rigid sequence

**Understand > Categorize:** Goal is true understanding, not just filling framework selection template
