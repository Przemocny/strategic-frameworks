# Risk Management Frameworks

## Czym jest?

Risk Management Frameworks to structured approaches które pomagają organizacjom systematycznie identify, assess, mitigate i monitor risks, zapewniając consistency w zarządzaniu uncertainties i aligning risk strategies z business objectives.

W 2026, risk management evolved z traditional compliance-driven approach do strategic capability embedding risk insights directly w business planning, capital allocation, operational resilience i strategic decision-making.

## Dla kogo jest?

- **CISOs i Security teams** - zarządzających cybersecurity risks
- **CROs (Chief Risk Officers)** - oversight corporate risk
- **Compliance teams** - ensuring regulatory adherence
- **IT teams** - managing technology i operational risks
- **Board of Directors** - risk governance i oversight
- **Project Managers** - identifying i mitigating project risks

## Co można z tego uzyskać?

1. **Enhanced decision-making** - Vital insights o potential risks i their impact
2. **Regulatory compliance** - Meet legal i industry requirements
3. **Operational resilience** - Better prepared dla disruptions
4. **Stakeholder confidence** - Demonstrate responsible management
5. **Financial protection** - Reduce likelihood i impact of losses

## Dlaczego warto z tego skorzystać?

- **2026 Trends** - Risk leaders shifting z reporting do shaping business decisions
- **Competitive advantage** - Quantify exposure rapidly, simulate scenarios instantly
- **ROI translation** - Risk presented z same precision jako financial planning
- **Multiple frameworks available** - NIST, ISO, COSO, FAIR - tailored strategies dla różnych industries
- **Integration z AI** - AI risk management frameworks dla emerging tech (2026)

## Główne Risk Management Frameworks

### 1. 🛡️ ISO 31000 - International Standard

**Organization:** International Organization for Standardization

**Co to jest:**
Internationally accepted enterprise risk management framework establishing principles dla organizations to integrate risk-based decision-making w governance, planning, management, reporting, policies, values i cultures.

**Key Principles (11):**
1. Creates i protects value
2. Integrated part of organizational processes
3. Part of decision-making
4. Explicitly addresses uncertainty
5. Systematic, structured i timely
6. Based on best available information
7. Tailored to organization
8. Human i cultural factors
9. Transparent i inclusive
10. Dynamic, iterative i responsive
11. Continual improvement

**Process:**
1. Communication i consultation
2. Establishing context
3. Risk assessment (identification, analysis, evaluation)
4. Risk treatment
5. Monitoring i review

**Best for:**
- Organizations seeking international standard
- General enterprise risk management
- Flexible, principles-based approach

**Latest:** ISO 31000:2018 (current version)

---

### 2. 🔐 NIST RMF - Cybersecurity Framework

**Organization:** National Institute of Standards and Technology (US)

**Full name:** NIST Risk Management Framework

**Co to jest:**
Comprehensive, flexible framework dla managing security i privacy risks, particularly w federal systems ale widely adopted w private sector.

**7-Step Process:**
1. **Prepare** - Essential activities dla managing security/privacy risks
2. **Categorize** - System i information based na impact analysis
3. **Select** - Security/privacy controls based na risk assessment
4. **Implement** - Controls i document how implemented
5. **Assess** - Controls to determine jeśli operating correctly
6. **Authorize** - Senior official makes risk-based decision
7. **Monitor** - Continuous monitoring of controls

**Cybersecurity Framework (CSF):**
5 Core Functions:
- **Identify** - Develop understanding of risk
- **Protect** - Develop i implement safeguards
- **Detect** - Develop activities to identify cybersecurity events
- **Respond** - Develop response plans
- **Recover** - Develop plans to restore capabilities

**Best for:**
- Government agencies (required dla federal)
- Cybersecurity-focused organizations
- Critical infrastructure

**2026 Status:** Widely adopted, continuous updates dla emerging threats

---

### 3. 📊 COSO - Enterprise Risk Management

**Organization:** Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission

**Co to jest:**
Framework integrating enterprise risk management (ERM) z strategy-setting i performance.

**COSO ERM Components:**

**5 Components:**
1. **Governance i Culture** - Tone at the top, commitment do core values
2. **Strategy i Objective-Setting** - Risk appetite, strategic planning
3. **Performance** - Risk identification, assessment, response
4. **Review i Revision** - Assessing substantial changes
5. **Information, Communication, Reporting** - Internal/external reporting

**20 Principles** under these 5 components

**Best for:**
- Enterprise-wide risk management
- Integrating risk w strategic planning
- Public companies (especially Sarbanes-Oxley compliance)
- Financial services

**Latest:** COSO ERM Framework (2017) - "Enterprise Risk Management—Integrating with Strategy and Performance"

---

### 4. 💰 FAIR - Factor Analysis of Information Risk

**Organization:** FAIR Institute

**Co to jest:**
Framework providing quantitative model dla cybersecurity i operational risk management. Focus na measuring risk w financial terms.

**Key Innovation:** Quantifies risk exposure - both **probability** of loss i **magnitude** of loss.

**Taxonomy:**

**Risk = Loss Event Frequency × Loss Magnitude**

Components:
- **Threat Event Frequency**
  - Contact frequency
  - Probability of action
- **Vulnerability**
- **Loss Magnitude**
  - Primary loss (immediate costs)
  - Secondary loss (consequential costs)

**Process:**
1. Identify scenario
2. Determine Loss Event Frequency
3. Determine Loss Magnitude
4. Derive Risk
5. Communicate results (usually in financial terms)

**Best for:**
- Organizations wanting quantified risk analysis
- Financial institutions
- Data-driven decision-making
- Justifying security investments w financial terms

---

### 5. 🏢 COBIT - IT Governance & Management

**Organization:** ISACA

**Full name:** Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies

**Co to jest:**
Framework dla governance i management of enterprise IT, integrating risk management w IT operations.

**Core Model:**

**40 Governance i Management Objectives** across:
- **Governance** (EDM - Evaluate, Direct, Monitor)
- **Management** (APO, BAI, DSS, MEA)

**6 Principles:**
1. Provide stakeholder value
2. Holistic approach
3. Dynamic governance system
4. Governance distinct z management
5. Tailored to enterprise needs
6. End-to-end governance system

**Best for:**
- IT governance
- Aligning IT z business objectives
- IT audit i compliance
- Digital transformation initiatives

**Latest:** COBIT 2019 (current version)

---

### 6. 🔍 OCTAVE - Operational Risk

**Organization:** Carnegie Mellon Software Engineering Institute

**Full name:** Operationally Critical Threat, Asset, and Vulnerability Evaluation

**Co to jest:**
Suite of tools, techniques i methods dla risk-based information security strategic assessment i planning.

**Variants:**
- **OCTAVE** - Large organizations
- **OCTAVE-S** - Small organizations (<100 people)
- **OCTAVE Allegro** - Streamlined, asset-focused

**3 Phases:**
1. **Build Asset-Based Threat Profiles** - Identify critical assets i threats
2. **Identify Infrastructure Vulnerabilities** - Evaluate IT infrastructure
3. **Develop Security Strategy i Plans** - Mitigation i response

**Best for:**
- Self-directed risk assessments
- Organizations bez dedicated security teams
- Asset-focused approach
- Operational technology environments

## 2026 Trendy w Risk Management

### 1. AI Risk Management Frameworks

**NIST AI RMF (2023):**
Framework dla managing risks of AI systems, addressing:
- Bias i fairness
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Security i safety

**Best Practices (2026):**
- Conduct AI-specific risk assessments
- Implement AI governance structures
- Monitor AI systems continuously
- Address ethical considerations

**Key firms:** OpenAI, Microsoft, Google implementing AI risk frameworks

### 2. From Reporting to Shaping Decisions

**Old model:** GRC-driven (Governance, Risk, Compliance) - reporting after the fact

**New model (2026):**
- **Financial impact quantification**
- **Capital allocation insights**
- **Operational resilience planning**
- **Strategic planning integration**

**Competitive advantage:** Teams które quantify exposure rapidly, simulate scenarios instantly, translate risk do ROI z precision

### 3. Real-time Risk Monitoring

**Technology enabling:**
- Continuous monitoring platforms
- AI-powered threat detection
- Automated compliance checking
- Dashboard i visualization tools

**Example tools:**
- ServiceNow GRC
- LogicManager
- Resolver
- MetricStream

### 4. Scenario-Based Risk Planning

Integration z Scenario Planning:
- Multiple future scenarios
- Risk exposure per scenario
- Strategic responses
- Early warning indicators

### 5. ESG Risk Integration

Environmental, Social, Governance risks now explicitly:
- Climate risk assessments
- Supply chain sustainability risks
- Social responsibility risks
- Governance structure risks

## Wybór właściwego frameworka

### Pytania:

**1. Industry i Regulation:**
- Healthcare → HIPAA, NIST
- Financial → COSO, SOX
- Government → NIST (required)
- General → ISO 31000

**2. Organization Size:**
- Small → OCTAVE-S, simplified approaches
- Medium → COBIT, ISO 31000
- Large → COSO, full NIST

**3. Focus Area:**
- Cybersecurity → NIST CSF, FAIR
- Enterprise-wide → COSO, ISO 31000
- IT Governance → COBIT
- Quantitative → FAIR

**4. Maturity Level:**
- Starting → ISO 31000 (principles-based)
- Intermediate → NIST, COBIT
- Advanced → FAIR (quantitative), COSO integrated

**5. Resources:**
- Limited → OCTAVE, ISO 31000
- Moderate → COBIT, NIST
- Extensive → COSO full implementation, FAIR modeling

## Combining Frameworks

**Recommended combinations:**

**Cybersecurity-focused:**
- NIST CSF (overall) + FAIR (quantification)

**Enterprise-wide:**
- ISO 31000 (principles) + COSO (integration)

**IT Governance:**
- COBIT (governance) + NIST (security)

**Compliance-heavy:**
- COSO (framework) + SOX, GDPR, HIPAA (regulations)

## Implementation Best Practices

### DO:
✅ Start z risk appetite definition
✅ Get executive buy-in i sponsorship
✅ Tailor framework do organization context
✅ Integrate z existing processes (nie separate)
✅ Use technology dla efficiency
✅ Train staff on framework i processes
✅ Regular reviews i updates
✅ Quantify risks w financial terms gdzie możliwe

### DON'T:
❌ Implement framework bez understanding "why"
❌ Copy-paste bez customization
❌ Treat jako compliance checkbox
❌ Ignore cultural i human factors
❌ Overcomplicate - start simple
❌ Forget continuous improvement
❌ Separate risk management od business decisions
