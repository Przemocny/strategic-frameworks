# SEEDS Model (Cognitive Biases Framework)

## Czym jest?

SEEDS Model to mnemonic framework dla kategoryzacji cognitive biases (błędów poznawczych) stworzony przez Buster Benson i John Manoogian III, popularyzowany przez work Bensona w 2016 roku. Model grupuje ponad 200 identified cognitive biases w **pięć głównych kategorii** based on underlying problems które biases "solve": **Social, Ego, Emotion, Data, Speed**.

Framework powstał jako response do overwhelming complexity Cognitive Bias Codex - zamiast pamiętać 200+ individual biases, SEEDS oferuje structure do rozumienia **dlaczego** te biases istnieją i **kiedy** jesteśmy podatni. Nie jest to tool do eliminacji biases (niemożliwe), ale do **awareness i mitigation** - recognize when bias może wpływać na decisions i compensate.

Kluczowa filozofia: **Cognitive biases nie są bugs - są features evolved aby pomóc nam survive** w information-overloaded, uncertain, fast-moving world. Ale te "shortcuts" often lead do predictable errors w modern decision-making contexts.

## Dla kogo jest?

- **Decision makers** - liderzy, managers podejmujący strategic/operational decisions
- **Product Managers** - rozumiejących user psychology i własne biases
- **Designers** - aware jak biases wpływają na user behavior
- **Marketers** - leveraging (ethically) lub protecting przeciwko biases
- **Każdy myślący człowiek** - improving personal decision quality

## Co można z tego uzyskać?

1. **Bias awareness** - recognize when you're likely to be biased
2. **Better decisions** - compensate dla known biases w important decisions
3. **Team effectiveness** - identify groupthink i collective biases
4. **Product design** - understand user mental models i shortcuts
5. **Communication skills** - frame messages accounting dla audience biases

## Dlaczego warto z tego skorzystać?

- **Simplifies complexity** - reduces 200+ biases do 5 memorable categories
- **Practical framework** - focuses na **why** biases exist, not just **what** they are
- **Universal application** - relevant w business, personal life, product design, investing
- **Evidence-based** - grounded w decades of behavioral economics i psychology research
- **Complementary** - enhances other frameworks (decision-making, strategy, design)

## Pięć Kategorii SEEDS

### S - Social Biases (Wpływy Społeczne)

**Core Problem:** Humans są social animals - musimy navigate group dynamics, conform dla survival, understand others' minds.

**When these biases activate:** W contexts involving other people, groups, social hierarchies.

#### Kluczowe biases w tej kategorii:

**1. Conformity Bias (Bias Konformizmu)**
**Definicja:** Tendency do agree z group opinion, nawet jeśli privately disagree.

**Dlaczego istnieje:** Evolutionary - dissenting from group = risk ostracism/danger
**Modern context:** Suppresses diverse viewpoints w meetings, creates groupthink

**Przykład:**
- **Business:** Team meeting gdzie wszyscy agree z CEO's plan, nikt nie voice concerns
- **Personal:** Agreeing z friends' restaurant choice mimo że chcesz coś innego

**Mitigation:**
- Anonymous feedback channels
- Devil's advocate roles
- "What would you do if you were CEO?" questions

**2. Halo Effect**
**Definicja:** Overall impression of person influences perception of specific traits.

**Przykład:**
- **Hiring:** Candidate from prestigious school assumed competent w all areas
- **Marketing:** Attractive spokesperson makes product seem better quality

**Mitigation:**
- Structured interviews z independent evaluations
- Blind assessments where possible

**3. In-group Favoritism**
**Definicja:** Preferring members of one's own group over outsiders.

**Przykład:**
- **Business:** Promoting people from your department vs other teams
- **Investing:** Overinvesting w local companies (home bias)

**Mitigation:**
- Diverse decision-making panels
- Objective criteria dla evaluation

**4. Authority Bias**
**Definicja:** Over-weighting opinions of authority figures.

**Przykład:**
- **Medicine:** Nurses not questioning doctor's obviously wrong prescription (Milgram)
- **Business:** Junior employee not challenging executive's flawed logic

**Mitigation:**
- Encourage "respectful dissent"
- Question credentials relevance do specific decision

#### Social Biases w praktyce:

**Context:** Corporate strategy meeting discussing entering new market.

**Biases at play:**
- **Conformity:** Junior members don't voice concerns bo CEO enthusiastic
- **Authority:** CEO's opinion carries disproportionate weight
- **In-group:** Team favors markets gdzie they have personal connections

**Better process:**
- Pre-meeting: Anonymous survey collecting concerns
- Meeting: Structured debate - assign roles (supporter, skeptic)
- Decision: Weighted voting based on expertise, nie seniority

### E - Ego Biases (Ochrona Ego)

**Core Problem:** Maintaining positive self-image jest psychological necessity - chronić self-esteem, feel competent, attribute success properly.

**When these biases activate:** W contexts involving self-evaluation, success/failure attribution, identity.

#### Kluczowe biases:

**1. Overconfidence Bias**
**Definicja:** Overestimating one's abilities, knowledge, lub accuracy of predictions.

**Variations:**
- **Overplacement:** Thinking you're better than others (better than average effect)
- **Overprecision:** Too certain about accuracy of beliefs
- **Overestimation:** Overestimating actual performance

**Przykład:**
- **Startups:** 90% founders think they'll succeed (reality: <10% do)
- **Investing:** Retail investors thinking they can beat market (most don't)
- **Project management:** Underestimating time/cost (planning fallacy)

**Mitigation:**
- **Pre-mortem:** "Assume project failed - why?"
- **Outside view:** Base rates, reference class forecasting
- **Track record review:** Historical accuracy of your predictions

**2. Self-Serving Bias**
**Definicja:** Attributing successes do yourself, failures do external factors.

**Przykład:**
- **Success:** "We hit target because of my brilliant strategy"
- **Failure:** "We missed target because market conditions changed"

**Mitigation:**
- Consistent attribution framework
- Post-mortems examining both luck i skill

**3. Dunning-Kruger Effect**
**Definicja:** Low-ability individuals overestimate competence; high-ability underestimate.

**Chart:**
```
Confidence
    |
    |   /\
    |  /  \___
    | /       \___
    |/            \___
    +-------------------> Competence
    Low           High
```

**Przykład:**
- **Novice investor:** Confident po 3 lucky trades
- **Expert:** Aware of complexity, humble

**Mitigation:**
- Seek feedback from experts
- Continuous learning mindset
- Recognize "I don't know what I don't know"

**4. Confirmation Bias (Ego aspect)**
**Definicja:** Seeking information that confirms existing beliefs.

**Ego connection:** Changing mind = admitting you were wrong = ego threat

**Przykład:**
- **Politics:** Only reading news that supports your views
- **Business:** CEO ignores data contradicting their strategy

**Mitigation:**
- **Steel man:** Argue strongest version of opposing view
- **Actively seek disconfirming evidence**
- "What would change my mind?"

#### Ego Biases w praktyce:

**Context:** Founder reviewing startup performance.

**Biased thinking:**
- "User growth is slow because market isn't ready (external)"
- "Our design awards prove we're building great product (overconfidence)"
- "That critical customer feedback is outlier (confirmation bias)"

**Debiased thinking:**
- "Let's examine: what decisions did we make that contributed?"
- "Awards dla design ≠ product-market fit. What do retention metrics say?"
- "Critical feedback might reveal systemic issue. Survey broader sample."

### E - Emotion Biases (Wpływ Emocji)

**Core Problem:** Emotions evolved jako rapid response system - guide decisions when deliberation too slow lub costly.

**When these biases activate:** Under stress, time pressure, strong emotional states.

#### Kluczowe biases:

**1. Loss Aversion**
**Definicja:** Pain of losing > pleasure of equivalent gain (~2x stronger).

**From:** Kahneman & Tversky's Prospect Theory

**Przykład:**
- **Investing:** Holding losing stocks too long (don't want to "realize loss")
- **Business:** Not killing failing projects (sunk cost fallacy)
- **Negotiation:** Overly focused na not losing vs finding gains

**Mitigation:**
- **Frame as opportunity cost:** "What else could we do with these resources?"
- **Kill criteria:** Define upfront when to exit

**2. Recency Bias**
**Definicja:** Over-weighting recent events vs historical data.

**Przykład:**
- **Investing:** Buying stocks after recent run-up (assuming trend continues)
- **Hiring:** Over-weighting last interview vs overall candidate assessment

**Mitigation:**
- Look at longer time horizons
- Historical baselines

**3. Availability Heuristic**
**Definicja:** Judging frequency/probability based on how easily examples come to mind.

**Przykład:**
- **Risk assessment:** Overestimating terrorism risk (vivid media coverage) vs heart disease (more common but less vivid)
- **Product:** Overweighting vocal minority user complaints

**Mitigation:**
- **Base rates:** Actual statistical frequency
- **Representative sampling**

**4. Affect Heuristic**
**Definicja:** Letting current emotion dictate judgment.

**Przykład:**
- **Angry:** Making rash decisions (firing employee impulsively)
- **Excited:** Overestimating upside (deal fever w M&A)

**Mitigation:**
- **Cool down period:** Sleep on big decisions
- **Emotional labeling:** "I notice I'm angry - not ideal state dla decision"

#### Emotion Biases w praktyce:

**Context:** CEO deciding whether to pivot startup after competitor launch.

**Emotion-driven decision:**
- **Fear:** Competitor launched → panic → rush pivot bez analysis
- **Loss aversion:** "We've invested 2 years w current direction, can't give up now"
- **Recency:** Last week's bad press → overweight significance

**Emotion-aware decision:**
- "I'm feeling fear - natural, but let's analyze objectively"
- "Sunk costs są irrelevant. What's best path forward from today?"
- "One bad week ≠ trend. Review 6-month data."

### D - Data Biases (Problemy z Informacją)

**Core Problem:** Overwhelmed przez information - musimy filter, simplify, find patterns w noise.

**When these biases activate:** Processing complex data, incomplete information, uncertainty.

#### Kluczowe biases:

**1. Anchoring Bias**
**Definicja:** Over-relying na first piece of information (anchor) when making decisions.

**Przykład:**
- **Salary negotiation:** First number mentioned sets anchor
- **Pricing:** Original price influences perception of discount ("Was $100, now $50!")
- **Estimates:** First estimate biases subsequent estimates

**Mitigation:**
- **Generate independent estimates** before seeing others'
- **Extreme anchors:** Consider multiple anchors, not just first
- **Awareness:** Recognize when you've been anchored

**2. Cherry Picking (Selection Bias)**
**Definicja:** Highlighting data supporting conclusion, ignoring contradicting data.

**Przykład:**
- **Marketing:** "9 out of 10 customers satisfied!" (ignoring how survey was conducted)
- **Presentations:** Showing only positive metrics

**Mitigation:**
- **Pre-register analysis plan:** Define metrics before collecting data
- **Present full picture:** Good i bad metrics

**3. Clustering Illusion**
**Definicja:** Seeing patterns w random data.

**Przykład:**
- **Investing:** "I've found pattern w stock movements!" (often randomness)
- **Sports:** "Hot hand" lub "streaks" (often random variance)

**Mitigation:**
- **Statistical testing:** Is pattern significant?
- **Understand randomness:** Humans terrible at recognizing random sequences

**4. Survivorship Bias**
**Definicja:** Focusing na "survivors" i ignoring those that didn't make it.

**Classic example:**
- **WWII planes:** Analyzing bullet holes na returning planes → reinforce where holes are
- **Correct:** Reinforce where holes AREN'T (planes hit there didn't return)

**Business example:**
- **Startup advice:** Learning only from successful founders (ignore failed founders' lessons)
- **Investing:** Studying successful companies (ignore bankruptcies)

**Mitigation:**
- **Include failed cases** w analysis
- **Ask:** "Who/what am I NOT seeing?"

#### Data Biases w praktyce:

**Context:** Analyzing customer churn data.

**Biased analysis:**
- **Anchoring:** Focus na 20% churn rate (industry benchmark anchored thinking)
- **Cherry picking:** Show cohorts gdzie retention improved, ignore deteriorating cohorts
- **Survivorship:** Survey only retained customers dla feedback

**Debiased analysis:**
- Review absolute churn trends, not just vs benchmark
- Report all cohorts, including worst performers
- **Critical:** Survey churned customers (they have answers!)

### S - Speed Biases (Skróty Poznawcze)

**Core Problem:** Limited time i cognitive resources - musimy make decisions quickly, conserve mental energy.

**When these biases activate:** Time pressure, decision fatigue, need dla rapid action.

#### Kluczowe biases:

**1. Representativeness Heuristic**
**Definicja:** Judging probability based on how similar something jest do prototype, ignoring base rates.

**Example:**
- **Linda problem:** "Linda is 31, philosophy major, concerned with social justice. Which is more probable: (A) Linda jest bank teller, (B) Linda jest bank teller i feminist?"
- **Most choose B** (seems representative) but **A is always more probable** (basic probability)

**Business example:**
- Founder from Stanford, articulate, confident → assume startup will succeed (ignoring base rate: most startups fail)

**Mitigation:**
- **Base rates first:** Start z statistical probability, then adjust
- **Bayesian thinking**

**2. Status Quo Bias**
**Definicja:** Preferring current state, resisting change even gdy change jest beneficial.

**Przykład:**
- **Default options:** Whatever is default jest chosen (organ donation rates)
- **Investing:** Not rebalancing portfolio (inertia)
- **Business:** "We've always done it this way"

**Mitigation:**
- **Zero-based thinking:** "If starting from scratch, would we choose this?"
- **Forced choice:** Remove default, require active decision

**3. Hyperbolic Discounting**
**Definicja:** Preferring smaller immediate rewards over larger delayed rewards.

**Example:**
- **$100 today vs $120 w rok** → most choose $100 (despite 20% return)
- **But $100 w 5 years vs $120 w 6 years** → most choose $120 (time distance reduces discounting)

**Business implications:**
- Short-termism (quarterly earnings vs long-term investment)
- Instant gratification features vs better long-term product

**Mitigation:**
- **Commitment devices:** Lock w long-term decisions
- **Visualize future self:** Make future benefits feel real

**4. Framing Effect**
**Definicja:** Drawing different conclusions from same information depending na how it's presented.

**Classic example:**
- **Surgery framing:**
  - "90% survival rate" → most choose surgery
  - "10% mortality rate" → many decline
  - **Same information**, different frame

**Business example:**
- "Save $10 na $100 purchase" (10%) feels better than "Lose $10 discount" - same outcome

**Mitigation:**
- **Reframe problem** multiple ways
- Consider absolute numbers, nie tylko percentages

#### Speed Biases w praktyce:

**Context:** Team deciding features dla next sprint.

**Speed-biased decision:**
- **Status quo:** "Keep building what we planned last quarter" (inertia)
- **Hyperbolic discounting:** Choose quick wins over strategic features
- **Framing:** "We'll lose competitive advantage!" (loss frame) vs "Opportunity to differentiate" (gain frame)

**Deliberate decision:**
- Zero-based: "Ignoring past plans, what should we build based on current data?"
- Long-term view: "What builds sustainable advantage vs quick metrics boost?"
- Multiple frames: Consider both upside (opportunity) i downside (risk)

## SEEDS w praktyce: Decision-Making Checklist

**Before important decision:**

### 1. Social Check:
- [ ] Am I conforming do group pressure?
- [ ] Am I over-weighting authority opinions?
- [ ] Is in-group favoritism influencing me?

### 2. Ego Check:
- [ ] Am I overconfident w my predictions?
- [ ] Am I attributing failures do external factors?
- [ ] Am I seeking only confirming evidence?

### 3. Emotion Check:
- [ ] What emotion am I feeling? (Name it)
- [ ] Is current emotion appropriate dla this decision?
- [ ] Am I over-weighting recent events?

### 4. Data Check:
- [ ] Have I been anchored by first number/idea?
- [ ] Am I cherry-picking data?
- [ ] Am I seeing patterns that might be random?
- [ ] What data am I NOT seeing? (Survivorship)

### 5. Speed Check:
- [ ] Am I relying on representativeness vs base rates?
- [ ] Am I sticking z status quo without justification?
- [ ] Am I choosing short-term over long-term?
- [ ] Have I considered multiple framings?

**If multiple YES answers → Slow down, get outside perspective, use structured decision process.**

## Debiasing Strategies (Practical)

### Individual Level:

**1. Awareness:**
- Study common biases
- Reflect on past decisions - identify bias patterns

**2. Pre-commitment:**
- Decision journals: Write criteria before deciding
- Pre-mortems: Imagine failure, work backwards

**3. Outside view:**
- Base rates, reference class forecasting
- "What would outsider recommend?"

**4. Cool-down periods:**
- Sleep on big decisions
- Separate information gathering from decision

### Team Level:

**1. Structured processes:**
- **Checklists** (like above)
- **Devil's advocate** roles
- **Red team / Blue team** debates

**2. Diverse perspectives:**
- Include people z different backgrounds
- Anonymous input channels

**3. Decision audits:**
- Review past decisions - what went right/wrong?
- Identify recurring bias patterns

### Organizational Level:

**1. Culture:**
- **Psychological safety** - safe do dissent
- **Growth mindset** - changing mind jest strength

**2. Systems:**
- **Blind resume reviews** (reduce bias)
- **Structured interviews** (reduce halo effect)
- **A/B testing culture** (data over opinions)

## SEEDS + Other Frameworks

**SEEDS complements:**

**Decision-making frameworks:**
- Identifies where biases skew analysis

**Strategy frameworks (Porter, SWOT):**
- Awareness of confirmation bias w competitive analysis

**Product design:**
- Understanding user biases (defaults, framing, social proof)

**Negotiation (BATNA, Principled):**
- Recognizing anchoring, loss aversion, framing effects

## Limitations of Debiasing

**Important caveat:** **Nie możesz eliminate biases** - są hardwired.

**Realistyczne cele:**
- **Awareness:** Recognize when bias likely present
- **Mitigation:** Use processes/tools to compensate
- **Acceptance:** Some biases są useful heuristics

**When biases są helpful:**
- **Intuition** w domain expertise (recognition-primed decisions)
- **Social cohesion** (some conformity beneficial)
- **Speed** when perfection unnecessary

**Wisdom:** Know when to trust gut (low stakes, time pressure, expertise) vs slow down (high stakes, novelty, complexity).
