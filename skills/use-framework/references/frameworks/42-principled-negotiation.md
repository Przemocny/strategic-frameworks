# Principled Negotiation (Getting to Yes)

## Czym jest?

Principled Negotiation, znany też jako **Interest-Based Negotiation** lub **Win-Win Negotiation**, to framework negocjacyjny stworzony przez Rogera Fishera i Williama Ury z Harvard Negotiation Project, opublikowany w książce "Getting to Yes" (1981). Framework powstał jako alternatywa dla dwóch tradycyjnych stylów negocjacji: **hard bargaining** (aggressive, positional) i **soft bargaining** (accommodating, yielding).

Centralną ideą jest separation **people from problem** i focus na **interests not positions**. Zamiast walczyć o fixed pie (win-lose), Principled Negotiation szuka creative solutions które maximize value dla both sides (win-win). Framework opiera się na czterech fundamentalnych principles i wprowadza koncepcję **BATNA** (Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement).

## Dla kogo jest?

- **Business negotiators** - kontrakty, partnerships, M&A
- **Sales professionals** - closing deals without burning relationships
- **Managers** - rozwiązywanie konfliktów w zespołach
- **Lawyers i mediatorzy** - facilitating agreements
- **Każdy kto negocjuje** - salary, purchase, family decisions

## Co można z tego uzyskać?

1. **Better outcomes** - creative solutions lepsze niż compromise
2. **Preserved relationships** - nie destroy relationships dla short-term win
3. **Efficient process** - less time w stalemates i posturing
4. **Objective criteria** - decisions based on fairness nie power
5. **Long-term agreements** - stable deals bo both sides satisfied

## Dlaczego warto z tego skorzystać?

- **Backed by research** - Harvard Negotiation Project, decades of field testing
- **Universal application** - działa w business, politics, personal negotiations
- **Przeciwwaga dla zero-sum thinking** - pokazuje że większość negocjacji nie musi być win-lose
- **Teachable framework** - clear principles, można trenować
- **Industry bible** - "Getting to Yes" sprzedane 15M+ kopii, taught w każdej business school

## Hard vs Soft vs Principled Negotiation

### Hard Bargaining (Positional, Competitive)
**Approach:** "I win, you lose"
- Take extreme positions i hold firm
- Exhaust other side
- Demand concessions as price of relationship
- Search for single answer: the one YOU will accept

**Problemy:**
- Damages relationships
- Inefficient (wastes time w posturing)
- Often leaves value on table
- Produces resentment

**Example:** "My price is $100K. Take it or leave it."

### Soft Bargaining (Accommodating)
**Approach:** "Let's be friends, I'll give in"
- Avoid conflict
- Make concessions readily
- Trust other side
- Accept losses to reach agreement

**Problemy:**
- You get exploited
- Build resentment on your side
- Doesn't actually preserve relationship (you feel used)
- Poor outcomes

**Example:** "Ok, $60K is fine even though I wanted $100K."

### Principled Negotiation (Interest-Based, Win-Win)
**Approach:** "Let's solve the problem together"
- Separate people from problem
- Focus on interests not positions
- Invent options for mutual gain
- Insist on objective criteria

**Benefits:**
- Better outcomes dla both sides
- Preserves (often strengthens) relationships
- Efficient
- Wise agreements

**Example:** "Help me understand what's most important to you in this deal. Maybe we can structure it in a way that works for both of us."

## Cztery Fundamentalne Principles

### Principle 1: Separate the People from the Problem

**Koncepcja:** People have emotions, biases, different perceptions. Problem jest problem - nie atakuj osoby.

**Dlaczego ważne:**
- **Perception:** Różne strony widzą sytuację różnie - to naturalne, nie złośliwość
- **Emotion:** Negocjacje wywołują strach, złość, frustration - adresuj emotions osobno od substance
- **Communication:** Misunderstanding destroys negotiations - clarity jest kluczowa

#### Tactics:

**Build relationship:**
- Arrive early, small talk, establish rapport
- "We're in this together against the problem"
- Acknowledge other side jako people with legitimate concerns

**Manage perceptions:**
- Rozumiej ich perspective: "Help me understand your view..."
- Nie zakładaj bad intentions: "I'm sure there's good reason..."
- Involve them w solution: "What would you suggest?"

**Control emotions (yours i theirs):**
- **Recognize i acknowledge** emotions: "I can see this is frustrating for you"
- **Don't react** do emotional outbursts: Stay calm, take break if needed
- **Allow venting:** Sometimes people need to express before they can move forward
- **Symbolic gestures:** Apology, acknowledgment, small concession to show good faith

**Communicate clearly:**
- **Active listening:** Paraphrase back: "So what I'm hearing is..."
- **Speak to be understood:** Clear, simple language
- **Speak about yourself, not them:** "I feel..." nie "You always..."

**Example:**
- **Bad:** "You're being completely unreasonable and greedy!"
- **Good:** "I understand you need to hit certain numbers. Help me understand what's driving that requirement, so we can see if there's a creative solution."

### Principle 2: Focus on Interests, Not Positions

**Koncepcja:** Position = what you say you want. Interest = why you want it. Interests are often compatible even gdy positions conflict.

**The Orange Story (classic):**
Two sisters fight over an orange (position: "I want the orange").
- Sister A wants to eat orange (interest: nutrition, taste)
- Sister B wants peel for baking (interest: orange zest for recipe)
- Positional compromise: Cut orange in half - both unsatisfied
- Interest-based solution: A gets all fruit, B gets all peel - both happy!

#### How to uncover interests:

**Ask "Why?":**
- "Why is that important to you?"
- "What's behind that requirement?"
- "Help me understand what you're trying to achieve"

**Ask "Why not?":**
- "What about this proposal doesn't work for you?"
- "What would happen if we did X?"

**Multiple interests:**
- Every side typically has multiple interests
- Some shared, some different, some conflicting
- **Shared interests** są foundation dla agreement
- **Different (compatible) interests** są opportunity dla trade

#### Types of interests:

**Substantive interests:** Money, terms, resources
**Process interests:** How decision gets made, timeline
**Relationship interests:** Ongoing partnership, reputation
**Psychological interests:** Feeling heard, being treated fairly, saving face

**Example (salary negotiation):**

**Position-based:**
- Employee: "I want $120K"
- Employer: "I can offer $100K"
- Compromise: $110K (both mildly satisfied)

**Interest-based:**
- Employee interests: Higher total compensation, career growth, work-life balance
- Employer interests: Stay w budget, retain talent, maintain pay equity
- Solution: $105K base + $10K signing bonus + extra week vacation + fast-track to senior role w 6 months (potentially worth more than $120K to employee, costs less than $120K ongoing dla employer)

### Principle 3: Invent Options for Mutual Gain

**Koncepcja:** Rozszerz pie zamiast tylko dzielić. Brainstorm creative solutions które create value dla both sides.

**Dlaczego trudne:**
- **Premature judgment:** Odrzucamy ideas za szybko
- **Search for single answer:** Myślimy że musi być jedna "right" answer
- **Fixed pie assumption:** Zakładamy że więcej dla nich = mniej dla nas
- **Solving "their" problem:** Myślimy tylko o swoich potrzebach

#### Process tworzenia opcji:

**Step 1: Separate inventing from deciding**
- Brainstorm session bez judgment
- Wild ideas welcome
- Quantity over quality initially
- "Yes, and..." nie "Yes, but..."

**Step 2: Broaden options on table**
- Vary strengths of agreement (from minimal to comprehensive)
- Change scope (more/less included)
- Different ways to split
- Consider different standards of fairness

**Step 3: Search for mutual gain**
- **Identify shared interests:** Build on these
- **Dovetail different interests:** Trade what's cheap for you ale valuable dla them
- **Ask for their preferences:** "Which of these options works best for you?"

**Step 4: Make their decision easy**
- Frame option w light of their interests
- Lower barriers (risk, uncertainty, hassle)
- Give them a "yes-able" proposal

#### Techniques dla inventing opcji:

**Looking for trades:**
- What's low cost dla Ciebie, high value dla nich?
- Example: Flexible payment schedule (low cost dla buyer, high value dla seller needing cash flow)

**Contingent agreements:**
- "If X happens, then Y"
- Useful gdy disagreement o future predictions
- Example: "If revenue exceeds $10M, earn-out triggers additional payment"

**Different currencies:**
- Money, time, resources, access, information, credibility
- Trade across currencies
- Example: Startup gives equity (cheap dla them early on) w exchange za mentorship/connections (valuable)

**Example (vendor negotiation):**

**Position battle:**
- Buyer: "Lower price to $80K"
- Seller: "Can't go below $100K"
- Stalemate

**Option invention:**
- Option A: $90K if pay upfront (seller gets cash flow, buyer gets discount)
- Option B: $95K + 3-year contract (seller gets predictability, buyer gets price certainty)
- Option C: $100K + seller includes training i support dla first year (same revenue dla seller, higher value dla buyer)
- Option D: $85K + buyer provides case study i testimonial (helps seller marketing)

### Principle 4: Insist on Using Objective Criteria

**Koncepcja:** Base agreement na fair standards niezależne od woli stron. Not "I want" vs "You want", ale "What's fair?"

**Dlaczego ważne:**
- **Legitimacy:** Decisions accepted when seen as fair
- **Efficient:** Mniej time w positional battle
- **Protects relationships:** Not personal (my will vs yours), ale objective
- **Creates precedent:** Consistent standards dla future

#### Examples of objective criteria:

**Market value:**
- "What do comparable properties sell for?"
- "What's market rate for this role?"
- Industry benchmarks, salary surveys

**Scientific standards:**
- Safety regulations, technical specifications
- "Building codes require X"

**Professional standards:**
- Ethical codes, industry best practices
- "Standard contract terms w this industry include..."

**Precedent:**
- "How did you handle similar situations?"
- Past agreements, case law

**Efficiency/costs:**
- "What's most cost-effective solution?"
- ROI analysis, cost-benefit

**Equal treatment:**
- "We should both have same terms"
- Fairness, reciprocity

#### How to use objective criteria:

**Frame każdą issue jako joint search for criteria:**
- "What would be fair way to determine X?"
- "What standards should we use to evaluate this?"

**Be reasonable, listen do their criteria:**
- "That's interesting standard. Can you explain rationale?"
- Consider their proposed criteria fairly

**Never yield do pressure, only principle:**
- **Pressure:** "This is my final offer, take it or leave it"
- **Your response:** "I appreciate the offer. Help me understand what makes that fair? Let's look at market comparables..."

**Example (real estate):**

**Without criteria (positional):**
- Seller: "I want $500K"
- Buyer: "I'll pay $400K"
- Haggling...

**With objective criteria:**
- "Let's look at comparable homes w neighborhood that sold w last 3 months"
- [Data shows: Similar homes sold $430-460K]
- "Given this data, what price makes sense?"
- More likely: Agreement around $440-450K based on specific features

## BATNA: Your Source of Power

**BATNA = Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement**

**Koncepcja:** Twoja best option jeśli negotiation fails. Your walk-away power.

### Dlaczego BATNA jest kluczowe:

- **Protects Cię** od accepting terms worse than your alternative
- **Empowers Cię** do negotiate confidently
- **Determines reservation point** - lowest you'll accept (seller) lub highest you'll pay (buyer)

### How to use BATNA:

**1. Identify Your BATNA:**
- Jeśli ta negotiation fails, what will you do?
- Example (job offer): Your BATNA może być current job, offer from other company, freelancing

**2. Improve Your BATNA:**
- **Strong BATNA = strong position**
- Example: If negotiating salary, getting competing offer massively improves BATNA

**3. Assess Their BATNA:**
- What are their alternatives?
- Weak BATNA = they need deal more

**4. Don't reveal weak BATNA:**
- If your BATNA is poor, nie pokazuj desperacji
- Focus na objective criteria i interests

**5. Use BATNA as threshold, not goal:**
- BATNA = walk-away point
- Aim higher through principled negotiation

**Example (buying car):**

**Your BATNA:** Can buy similar car at different dealership dla $25K
**Your reservation point:** Won't pay more than $25K
**This negotiation:** Try to get best deal below $25K through inventing opcji, objective criteria

**Their BATNA:** Can sell to other buyer (potentially)
**Their likely reservation:** Won't sell below certain price (invoice + margin)

**Negotiation:**
- You don't say "I have another offer at $25K" immediately (kills room)
- You explore interests: "I want reliable car, good financing. You want sale. What can we work out?"
- You use criteria: "Kelly Blue Book shows fair price at $23K. What justifies higher?"
- If stuck above $25K: "I appreciate offer, but I have other options at $25K. Can you match?"

## Handling Difficult Negotiators

### When they use positional tactics:

**Tactic: Extreme demands**
- **Don't mirror:** Don't respond z extreme counter
- **Reframe:** "That's one option. Let's explore what makes sense based on..."

**Tactic: Personal attacks**
- **Don't take bait:** Recognize jako tactic
- **Reframe:** "Let's focus on solving the problem..."

**Tactic: Good cop / Bad cop**
- **Recognize pattern:** "I notice one of you is reasonable, one is hard. Let's all focus on interests..."

**Tactic: Take it or leave it**
- **Don't fold:** "I need to understand what makes that fair before I can agree"
- **Surface BATNA implicitly:** "I'd like to reach agreement, but only if it makes sense for both sides"

### When they won't budge:

**Ask questions:**
- "Help me understand why that's important"
- "What would happen if we tried X instead?"

**Invent opcje together:**
- "What if we structured it differently?"

**Use objective criteria:**
- "Let's look at how similar situations were handled"

**Take break:**
- "Let's both think about this i reconvene tomorrow"

## Principled Negotiation w praktyce: Przykład

**Scenario:** Negocjujesz contract jako freelance consultant z corporate client.

### Their opening:
"We can pay $100/hour, 20 hours/week, 3-month contract. Non-negotiable."

### Traditional response (positional):
"I need $150/hour minimum."
→ Likely: Stalemate lub grudging compromise

### Principled Negotiation approach:

**1. Separate people from problem:**
- "Thanks for the offer. I'm excited about the project i want to find structure that works dla both of us."

**2. Uncover interests:**
- "Help me understand your constraints on budget i hours?"
- [They reveal: Fixed budget $24K dla 3 months, need deliverable by end Q2]
- Your interests: Higher effective pay, interesting work, flexibility, potential ongoing relationship

**3. Invent opcje:**
- Option A: $120/hour, 20 hours/week, but 4-month contract (same total $28.8K, spread cost)
- Option B: $100/hour, 30 hours/week first month (intensive start), then 15 hours maintenance (same total, different timeline)
- Option C: $110/hour, 20 hours/week, + performance bonus if deliver early (value-based)
- Option D: $100/hour now, but Right of First Refusal na future projects (builds pipeline)

**4. Objective criteria:**
- "Market rate dla this expertise w our region is $120-150/hour according to [industry report]"
- "If we structure as fixed-price project instead of hourly, what budget makes sense?"

**5. Your BATNA:**
- You have another potential client offering $130/hour
- This improves your position - you can walk if not reasonable

**Outcome:** Much higher likelihood of creative solution than positional battle.

## Kluczowe osoby

### Roger Fisher
Harvard Law professor, założyciel Harvard Negotiation Project (1979). Współautor "Getting to Yes" (1981), which became bestseller i zdefiniował modern negotiation theory. Zmarł 2012.

### William Ury
Antropolog, co-founder Harvard Negotiation Project. Współautor "Getting to Yes" i autor "Getting Past No" (dealing with difficult people). Active speaker i mediator w international conflicts.

### Bruce Patton
Trzeci współautor "Getting to Yes" (later editions), Deputy Director Harvard Negotiation Project. Focused na teaching i applying framework.
