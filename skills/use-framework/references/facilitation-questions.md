# Facilitation Questions Library

This library contains questions for facilitating framework workshops. Use these to draw out user thinking, challenge assumptions, and drive toward insights.

**Usage:** Ask questions to FACILITATE user's thinking, not to interrogate. Questions should help user discover insights, not feel tested.

---

## Universal Facilitation Questions

Use these across all frameworks and phases.

### Opening Questions (Start of Any Phase)

**Set context:**
- "Let's move to [Phase Name]. The goal here is to [objective]"
- "This phase explores [what]. Ready to dive in?"
- "What comes to mind when I say [phase topic]?"

### Depth Probing

**Get specific:**
- "Can you give me a concrete example?"
- "What does that look like in practice?"
- "Can you be more specific about [vague statement]?"
- "Walk me through exactly how that works"

**Challenge surface answers:**
- "Tell me more about that"
- "What's beneath that?"
- "Go deeper - why is that?"
- "What's really driving this?"

### Connection-Making

**Link insights:**
- "How does this relate to what you said earlier about [X]?"
- "I'm noticing a pattern: [theme]. Do you see that too?"
- "This connects to [earlier insight], doesn't it?"
- "What's the common thread here?"

### Assumption-Testing

**Challenge thinking:**
- "What are you assuming here?"
- "What if that assumption is wrong?"
- "How do you know that's true?"
- "What evidence do you have for that?"
- "Could the opposite be true?"

### Consequence Exploration

**Second-order thinking:**
- "Then what happens?"
- "And after that?"
- "What's the ripple effect?"
- "What's the consequence of that consequence?"

### Synthesis

**Pull together:**
- "What's the key insight from this phase?"
- "If you had to summarize in one sentence...?"
- "What's most important here?"
- "What changed in your thinking?"

## Strategic Framework Questions

For Porter's Five Forces, Blue Ocean, SWOT, Wardley Mapping, etc.

### Competitive Analysis

**Understanding competition:**
- "Who are your direct competitors?"
- "Who might compete with you in 2-3 years?"
- "What's different about your top 3 competitors?"
- "Why haven't competitors solved this already?"
- "What stops new entrants from copying you?"

**Competitive advantage:**
- "What can you do that others can't?"
- "Why would customers choose you over [competitor]?"
- "What's your unfair advantage?"
- "How defensible is your position?"
- "What would it take for a competitor to match you?"

### Market Dynamics

**Market forces:**
- "What's changing in your market?"
- "What trends are shaping your industry?"
- "Where is power concentrated?" (suppliers, buyers, you)
- "What forces are getting stronger/weaker?"
- "How is the competitive game changing?"

**Market opportunity:**
- "How big is this market opportunity?"
- "Is the market growing, stable, or shrinking?"
- "What's your realistic share of this market?"
- "Are you in the right market, or should you look elsewhere?"

### Strategic Positioning

**Differentiation:**
- "How are you meaningfully different?"
- "What makes you unique?"
- "Can customers see the difference?"
- "Is this difference sustainable?"

**Strategic choices:**
- "What are you optimizing for?" (cost, quality, speed, innovation)
- "What are you explicitly NOT competing on?"
- "Where are you choosing not to play?"
- "What strategic bets are you making?"

**Blue Ocean specific:**
- "What factors does your industry take for granted?"
- "What could you eliminate that the industry assumes is necessary?"
- "What could you reduce way below industry standard?"
- "What could you raise way above industry standard?"
- "What could you create that the industry has never offered?"

### Organizational Alignment (McKinsey 7S, etc.)

**System elements:**
- "What's your strategy? Is it clear and communicated?"
- "What's your organizational structure? Does it support strategy?"
- "What systems and processes do you have?"
- "What skills does your team have?"
- "What's your management style?"
- "What staff do you have? Right people?"
- "What shared values unite the organization?"

**Alignment:**
- "Are these 7 elements aligned or conflicting?"
- "Where's the biggest misalignment?"
- "What would need to change to align these?"

## Mental Model Questions

For Mental Models, First Principles, Second-Order Thinking, Inversion, etc.

### First Principles

**Breaking down:**
- "What are you actually trying to accomplish?"
- "What are your assumptions about how this must work?"
- "Which of these are fundamental truths vs conventions?"
- "What HAS to be true?" (physics, math, human nature)
- "What's just 'how it's always been done'?"

**Rebuilding:**
- "If you started from scratch, knowing only the fundamentals, how would you do this?"
- "Ignore how others do it. What's the best way?"
- "What if you couldn't use [conventional approach]?"
- "What would Elon Musk do here?" (question everything)

### Second-Order Thinking

**Consequences of consequences:**
- "If you do X, what happens?"
- "Then what?"
- "And then?"
- "What happens 5 steps down the chain?"
- "What's the long-term effect no one is seeing?"

**Unintended consequences:**
- "What could go wrong indirectly?"
- "Who's affected that you're not considering?"
- "What ripple effects might occur?"
- "What's the second-order impact on [stakeholder]?"

### Inversion

**Thinking backwards:**
- "What would guarantee failure?"
- "What must you avoid at all costs?"
- "Instead of asking how to succeed, how would you definitely fail?"
- "What's the opposite of your approach?"
- "What if you're wrong about [key assumption]?"

**Avoiding failure:**
- "What kills companies like yours?"
- "What mistakes do others make that you can avoid?"
- "What's the worst thing that could happen?"
- "How do you make sure that doesn't happen?"

### Mental Models (Munger)

**Multi-disciplinary thinking:**
- "How would an economist view this?"
- "What would a biologist notice?"
- "How does psychology explain this behavior?"
- "What physical laws apply here?"
- "What's the systems perspective?"

**Applying models:**
- "What mental models apply to this situation?"
- "Are you seeing this through the right lens?"
- "What framework would Charlie Munger use?"
- "What models am I NOT using that I should?"

### Circle of Competence

**Boundaries of knowledge:**
- "What do you actually know vs what do you think you know?"
- "Where are the edges of your expertise?"
- "What's outside your circle of competence here?"
- "Should you be making this decision, or should someone else?"
- "How could you expand your competence here?"

### Regret Minimization

**Long-term perspective:**
- "Imagine you're 80 years old, looking back. Do you regret this choice?"
- "What will your 80-year-old self wish you had done?"
- "Regret of action (doing it) vs regret of inaction (not doing it) - which is worse?"
- "What would be harder to explain to your older self?"
- "What will you wonder 'what if' about?"

## Decision Framework Questions

For OODA Loop, Pre-Mortem, Eisenhower Matrix, Cynefin, etc.

### Option Generation

**Exploring alternatives:**
- "What are all your options?"
- "What else could you do?"
- "What option are you not seeing?"
- "What would you do if you couldn't do [preferred option]?"
- "What's the opposite approach?"

### Option Evaluation

**Comparing choices:**
- "What's the upside of each option?"
- "What's the downside?"
- "Which is most reversible?"
- "Which has the highest expected value?"
- "Which aligns with your values/strategy?"

**Trade-offs:**
- "You can optimize for X or Y, not both. Which matters more?"
- "What do you give up with option A?"
- "What's the cost of option B?"
- "Which trade-off can you live with?"

### Risk Assessment

**Understanding risk:**
- "What could go wrong?"
- "How likely is the downside?"
- "How bad would it be if it fails?"
- "Is this risk acceptable?"
- "What's your backup plan?"

**Managing risk:**
- "How can you reduce the risk?"
- "How can you test this cheaply?"
- "What early warning signs should you watch?"
- "At what point do you pull the plug?"

### Pre-Mortem Specific

**Imagining failure:**
- "It's 12 months from now. Your plan failed completely. What happened?"
- "Tell me the story of how this failed"
- "What warning signs were there that you ignored?"
- "Why didn't you see this coming?"
- "What's the most embarrassing failure scenario?"

**Identifying risks:**
- "What's most likely to go wrong?"
- "What failure would hurt the most?"
- "What hidden risks aren't you considering?"
- "What assumption, if wrong, would doom this?"

### OODA Loop Specific

**Observe:**
- "What's happening in your environment?"
- "What signals are you seeing?"
- "What's changing?"
- "What data are you collecting?"

**Orient:**
- "How do you interpret these observations?"
- "What's your mental model of the situation?"
- "What biases might be affecting your perception?"
- "How would someone else interpret this?"

**Decide:**
- "What are your options?"
- "Which do you choose?"
- "Why this over alternatives?"

**Act:**
- "What specific action will you take?"
- "When will you act?"
- "How will you measure results?"
- "When do you loop back to Observe?"

### Cynefin Specific

**Domain assessment:**
- "Is cause-and-effect clear here?" (Simple/Clear)
- "Can experts analyze and determine best practice?" (Complicated)
- "Is the situation unpredictable with patterns emerging?" (Complex)
- "Is it chaotic with no discernible patterns?" (Chaotic)

**Appropriate response:**
- If Clear: "What's the best practice?"
- If Complicated: "What expertise is needed?"
- If Complex: "What experiments can you run?"
- If Chaotic: "What's the quickest action to stabilize?"

### Prioritization (Eisenhower, Pareto)

**Eisenhower Matrix:**
- "Is this urgent or not urgent?"
- "Is this important or not important?"
- Urgent + Important: "Do this now"
- Important + Not Urgent: "Schedule this"
- Urgent + Not Important: "Delegate this"
- Not Urgent + Not Important: "Delete this"

**Pareto Principle:**
- "What's the 20% that drives 80% of results?"
- "What has the most leverage?"
- "What should you do more of?"
- "What should you stop doing?"
- "Where's the biggest bang for buck?"

## Innovation Framework Questions

For Design Thinking, Jobs-to-be-Done, Lean Startup, Six Thinking Hats

### User Understanding (Empathy)

**Deep user needs:**
- "Who exactly is the user?" (be specific, not "everyone")
- "What are they trying to accomplish?"
- "What frustrates them about current solutions?"
- "What do they really value?" (not what they say)
- "What job are they 'hiring' your product to do?"

**Observing users:**
- "What have you observed users actually doing?"
- "What surprised you when watching them?"
- "What workarounds have they created?"
- "What do they do that they don't say?"

### Problem Definition

**Framing the problem:**
- "What's the REAL problem here?"
- "Whose problem is this?"
- "How would the user describe the problem?"
- "Complete this: [User] needs [need] because [insight]"
- "How might we [solve problem]?"

### Jobs-to-be-Done Specific

**Job identification:**
- "When do customers 'hire' your product?"
- "What progress are they trying to make?"
- "What's the job beneath the job?"

**Job dimensions:**
- "What's the functional job?" (what they're trying to do)
- "What's the emotional job?" (how they want to feel)
- "What's the social job?" (how they want to be seen)

**Competition:**
- "What else do they 'hire' for this job?"
- "What non-obvious alternatives exist?"
- "What did they do before your product existed?"

### Ideation

**Generating ideas:**
- "What are 10 ways to solve this?"
- "What's a wild, crazy idea?"
- "What would [company X] do?"
- "What if budget was unlimited?"
- "What if you had to solve this for $100?"

**Exploring possibilities:**
- "What else? And what else?"
- "Go weirder"
- "What's the opposite approach?"
- "What would make users say 'wow'?"

**Converging:**
- "Which ideas are most promising?"
- "Which addresses the core need?"
- "Which could you test quickly?"
- "Which excites you most?"

### Prototyping

**Minimum viable:**
- "What's the absolute minimum to test your hypothesis?"
- "What can you fake instead of build?"
- "How can you test this without code?"
- "What prototype takes 1 day vs 1 month?"

**Testing approach:**
- "Who will test this?"
- "What do you want to learn?"
- "What would make you pivot?"
- "What's the riskiest assumption to test first?"

### Lean Startup Specific

**Build-Measure-Learn:**
- "What's your hypothesis?"
- "What's the riskiest assumption?"
- "What's the minimum to test that assumption?"
- "What metric matters?"
- "What did you learn?"
- "Pivot or persevere?"

**Validated learning:**
- "What did users actually do?" (not say)
- "What surprised you?"
- "What was your prediction vs reality?"
- "What's your next experiment?"

## Operational Framework Questions

For Theory of Constraints, OKR, Pareto, Systems Thinking

### System Understanding

**Mapping the system:**
- "What's the system or process?"
- "What are the steps?"
- "What's the input? What's the output?"
- "Where do things flow smoothly? Where do they get stuck?"

**Interconnections:**
- "How do these parts connect?"
- "If you change X, what happens to Y?"
- "What's the feedback loop?"
- "Where are the dependencies?"

### Theory of Constraints Specific

**Finding constraint:**
- "Where's the bottleneck?"
- "What's the slowest step?"
- "Where does work pile up?"
- "What's limiting throughput?"
- "How do you know this is the real constraint?"

**Exploiting constraint:**
- "How can you get more from this bottleneck?"
- "Can you eliminate downtime at the constraint?"
- "Can you improve quality at the constraint?"

**Subordinating:**
- "What should run at the constraint's pace?"
- "What's pointless to optimize if it's not the constraint?"

**Elevating:**
- "How do you permanently fix this constraint?"
- "What investment would remove the bottleneck?"
- "What's the next constraint after this one?"

### OKR Specific

**Objectives:**
- "What do you want to achieve this quarter?"
- "What would great look like?"
- "Is this ambitious but achievable?"
- "Is this qualitative and inspirational?"

**Key Results:**
- "How will you measure success?"
- "What metrics matter?"
- "What's the baseline? What's the target?"
- "Are these metrics leading indicators or lagging?"

**Alignment:**
- "How do team OKRs connect to company OKRs?"
- "Do these OKRs support the strategy?"
- "What's not in your OKRs that should be?"

### Metrics & Measurement

**Defining metrics:**
- "What should you measure?"
- "What's the baseline?"
- "What's good? What's great?"
- "What's a realistic target?"

**Leading vs lagging:**
- "Is this a leading indicator (predicts future) or lagging (reports past)?"
- "Can you act on this metric?"
- "How quickly does this metric respond to changes?"

**Metric quality:**
- "Can this metric be gamed?"
- "Does improving this metric actually help the business?"
- "What's the unintended consequence of optimizing this?"

### Pareto Analysis

**Finding the vital few:**
- "What's the 20% that drives 80% of [results/problems/value]?"
- "What has the most leverage?"
- "What should you do more of?"
- "What should you stop doing entirely?"

**Prioritization:**
- "If you could only improve one thing, what would it be?"
- "What gives the most bang for your buck?"
- "Where's the low-hanging fruit?"

## Six Thinking Hats Questions

For structured perspective-taking.

### White Hat (Facts)

**Objective information:**
- "What data do we have?"
- "What facts are known?"
- "What information is missing?"
- "What evidence supports this?"

### Red Hat (Emotions)

**Intuition and feelings:**
- "What's your gut feeling?"
- "How do you feel about this?"
- "What's your emotional reaction?"
- "What feels right or wrong?"

### Black Hat (Caution)

**Risks and problems:**
- "What could go wrong?"
- "What are the risks?"
- "Why might this fail?"
- "What's the downside?"

### Yellow Hat (Benefits)

**Positive perspective:**
- "What's good about this?"
- "What are the benefits?"
- "Why could this succeed?"
- "What's the upside?"

### Green Hat (Creativity)

**New ideas:**
- "What are alternative approaches?"
- "What if we tried [wild idea]?"
- "How could we think about this differently?"
- "What's a creative solution?"

### Blue Hat (Process)

**Meta-thinking:**
- "What have we learned so far?"
- "What should we think about next?"
- "What's our conclusion?"
- "What's the action plan?"

## Facilitation Best Practices

### Question Design

**Good questions are:**
✅ Open-ended (not yes/no)
✅ Specific to user's situation
✅ Genuinely curious
✅ Forward-moving
✅ One question at a time

**Bad questions are:**
❌ Leading ("Don't you think X?")
❌ Compound ("What about A and B and C?")
❌ Vague ("What do you think?")
❌ Testing ("What's the definition of X?")
❌ Rhetorical ("You know what I mean?")

### Delivery

**How to ask:**
- Clear and simple language
- Pause after asking (let them think)
- One question at a time
- Follow user's answer, don't stick to script
- Ask follow-up based on their response

**Active listening:**
- Actually hear the answer
- Don't just wait to ask next question
- Notice what they emphasize
- Pay attention to energy and emotion
- Build on what they share

### When to Ask What

**Early in workshop:**
- Open, exploratory questions
- Build context and understanding
- "Tell me about..."

**Middle of workshop:**
- Specific, probing questions
- Challenge and deepen thinking
- "What's beneath that?"

**Late in workshop:**
- Synthesis questions
- Connect insights
- "What's the pattern?"

**End of workshop:**
- Action questions
- Next steps
- "What will you do?"

## Remember

**Questions are your primary facilitation tool**

Use them to:
- Draw out user's thinking
- Challenge assumptions
- Create insights
- Connect ideas
- Drive toward action

**Listen more than you talk**
- Best facilitators ask great questions then listen
- User should talk 70%, you talk 30%

**Adapt questions to user**
- Analytical users: More "why" questions
- Action-oriented users: More "how" and "what" questions
- Struggling users: More examples and structure

**Follow the interesting threads**
- Don't stick rigidly to question list
- When user says something interesting, explore it
- Best insights come from following energy
