# ADKAR Model

## Czym jest?

ADKAR Model to framework zarządzania zmianą na **poziomie indywidualnym** stworzony przez Jeffa Hiatta, założyciela Prosci, w 1996 roku. ADKAR to akronim od pięciu sequential building blocks które individual musi przejść aby successful change occurred: **Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement**.

W przeciwieństwie do organizational change models (jak Kotter 8-Step), ADKAR koncentruje się na **individual person** - bo ultimately organizacja nie zmienia się, **people się zmieniają**. Model powstał z badań Prosci obejmujących ponad 900 organizacji i identyfikuje że 70% change failures wynika z **employee resistance i management behaviors**, nie z technical issues.

Kluczowa filozofia: **Change happens one person at a time**. Możesz mieć perfect strategy, ale jeśli individuals nie przejdą przez wszystkie 5 stages, change fails.

## Dla kogo jest?

- **Change Managers** - zarządzających people side of change
- **HR Leaders** - wspierających organizational transitions
- **Managers i Supervisors** - prowadzących swoje teams przez zmianę
- **Project Managers** - rozumiejących dlaczego adoption fails
- **Individual contributors** - navigating change w swojej roli

## Co można z tego uzyskać?

1. **Diagnosis resistance** - identify GDZIE exactly individual jest stuck
2. **Targeted interventions** - address specific barrier zamiast generic "communication"
3. **Individual-level planning** - customize approach dla różnych people/groups
4. **Measure progress** - assess gdzie każda osoba jest w journey
5. **Higher adoption rates** - systematic approach do people side increases success

## Dlaczego warto z tego skorzystać?

- **Evidence-based** - derived from 900+ organizational change studies
- **Simple i memorable** - 5 sequential stages, easy to understand
- **Diagnostic power** - quickly identify gdzie resistance occurs
- **Universal** - applies do każdego change (tech, process, cultural, structural)
- **Complementary** - works with organizational models (Kotter, Lewin)

## Pięć Elementów ADKAR

### A - Awareness (Świadomość)

**Pytanie:** Czy rozumiem DLACZEGO zmiana jest konieczna?

**Definicja:** Understanding natury zmiany, dlaczego zmiana się dzieje, i risk of not changing.

#### Co tworzy Awareness:

**Business reasons:**
- External factors (competition, market, technology, regulations)
- Internal factors (performance gaps, inefficiencies, strategic goals)
- Consequences of NOT changing

**Personal reasons:**
- "What does this mean dla mnie?"
- Impact na moją rolę, zespół, karierę

#### Bariery do Awareness:

- **Lack of information:** Nikt nie wyjaśnił dlaczego zmiana się dzieje
- **Misinformation:** Słyszeli nieprawdziwe reasons lub rumors
- **Disagree with need:** Rozumieją reason ale nie zgadzają się ("Our current way is fine")

#### Jak budować Awareness:

**Communication strategies:**
- **Clear, consistent messaging** o business case
- **Multiple channels:** Email, town halls, 1-on-1s, videos
- **Repetition:** Say it many times różnymi ways
- **Two-way dialogue:** Not just broadcast - listen to concerns

**Show data:**
- Performance metrics showing problem
- Customer feedback, market trends
- Competitor analysis

**Make it personal:**
- "Here's jak to affects YOUR work"
- Connect organizational need do individual impact

**Przykład:**
- **Change:** Moving to new CRM system
- **Awareness building:** "Current system crashes 2x/week, losing customer data. Competitors respond faster. New CRM will prevent data loss i speed response time."

**Assessment question:** "On scale 1-5, how much do you understand WHY we're changing?" (If <3, need more Awareness work)

### D - Desire (Chęć)

**Pytanie:** Czy CHCĘ wspierać i uczestniczyć w zmianie?

**Definicja:** Personal decision i commitment do support i engage w change.

**Kluczowy insight:** **Awareness ≠ Desire**. Możesz rozumieć dlaczego zmiana jest needed (Awareness), ale still not want to do it (brak Desire).

#### Co wpływa na Desire:

**Personal factors:**
- **WIIFM (What's In It For Me?):** Co zyskam?
- **Loss aversion:** Co stracę? (familiar tools, status, comfort)
- **Trust w leadership:** Czy wierzę że liderzy mają moje best interests?
- **Past experiences:** Czy previous changes były successful/painful?

**Organizational factors:**
- **Consequences:** Rewards dla adoption, consequences dla resistance
- **Role models:** Czy respected peers/leaders embrace change?
- **Culture:** Czy organization historically rewards change lub status quo?

#### Bariery do Desire:

- **Fear:** Strach przed nieznanym, failure, redundancy
- **Comfort zone:** Obecny sposób jest known i comfortable
- **Not convinced:** Don't see personal benefit
- **Distrust:** Don't trust leaders' motives
- **Change fatigue:** "Another change? Last three failed..."

#### Jak budować Desire:

**Address WIIFM:**
- **Articulate personal benefits:** Career growth, easier work, new skills
- **Reduce personal losses:** Address fears, provide support

**Sponsorship i role models:**
- **Visible leader support:** Leaders must actively champion
- **Peer champions:** Respected colleagues advocating

**Incentives i consequences:**
- **Align rewards:** Performance reviews, bonuses tied to adoption
- **Clear consequences:** What happens jeśli don't adopt? (Not threats, but clarity)

**Build trust:**
- **Transparent communication:** Honest about challenges
- **Involve people:** Input w how change is implemented
- **Address past failures:** "Here's what we learned, here's how this time is different"

**Przykład:**
- **Change:** New CRM system
- **Desire building:**
  - WIIFM: "You'll spend 30% less time on data entry, more on selling"
  - Address fears: "We'll have 3 months training, support team available"
  - Incentives: Q2 bonuses tied to CRM adoption
  - Role model: Top sales rep publicly embraces system

**Assessment:** "On scale 1-5, how motivated are you to adopt this change?" (If <3, need Desire work)

**CRITICAL:** You can't force Desire. Ale możesz create environment where Desire naturally develops.

### K - Knowledge (Wiedza)

**Pytanie:** Czy wiem JAK się zmienić?

**Definicja:** Understanding how do change - information, training, education needed to implement change.

#### Typy Knowledge:

**Knowledge o change:**
- What is changing?
- How will new state look?
- What's timeline?

**Knowledge jak change:**
- **During transition:** How do I get from old to new?
- **New steady-state:** How do I work w new way?
- Skills, processes, tools

#### Bariery do Knowledge:

- **No training provided:** Expected to "figure it out"
- **Poor training:** Generic, not relevant, too early/late
- **Information overload:** Too much at once
- **Wrong format:** Training doesn't match learning styles

#### Jak dostarczyć Knowledge:

**Training programs:**
- **Just-in-time:** Right before they need to use
- **Hands-on:** Practice, not just lecture
- **Role-based:** Customized dla different jobs
- **Ongoing:** Not one-and-done

**Resources:**
- **Job aids:** Quick reference guides, cheat sheets
- **Documentation:** Updated procedures, SOPs
- **FAQs:** Common questions answered
- **Help desk:** Support when stuck

**Coaching i mentoring:**
- **Peer buddies:** Someone who learned already helps
- **Super users:** Power users available dla questions
- **Manager coaching:** 1-on-1 support

**Przykład:**
- **Change:** New CRM system
- **Knowledge delivery:**
  - Week before launch: 2-hour hands-on training per role
  - Day 1: Job aids at každdego desk
  - Ongoing: Weekly "office hours" z trainer
  - Champions w each team dla peer support

**Assessment:** "On scale 1-5, how confident are you że wiesz how to use new system?" (If <3, need more Knowledge)

**Common mistake:** Organizations often OVER-invest w Knowledge (training programs) i UNDER-invest w Awareness i Desire. All training in world doesn't help jeśli people don't understand why lub don't want to change.

### A - Ability (Umiejętność)

**Pytanie:** Czy potrafię wykonać zmianę w praktyce?

**Definicja:** Realized performance - turning Knowledge into actual behavior/skill.

**Kluczowy insight:** **Knowledge ≠ Ability**. Możesz "know" how (Knowledge), but still struggle to actually DO it (Ability).

**Analogy:** Możesz wiedzieć teorię jak jeździć na rowerze (Knowledge), but first time actually riding wymaga practice i może upaść (building Ability).

#### Bariery do Ability:

**Psychological barriers:**
- **Fear of failure:** "What if I mess up?"
- **Old habits:** Muscle memory z old way
- **Confidence:** "I don't think I can do this"

**Physical/mental barriers:**
- **Time to practice:** Busy z current work, no time dla new way
- **Cognitive load:** New way is harder/slower initially
- **Talent limitations:** Some people struggle more

**Environmental barriers:**
- **Lack of support:** No one to ask when stuck
- **Inadequate tools:** System crashes, poor design
- **Competing priorities:** Pressured do deliver using old way

#### Jak budować Ability:

**Practice opportunities:**
- **Sandbox environments:** Practice bez real consequences
- **Pilot groups:** Small group tries first, learns, teaches others
- **Time allocation:** Dedicated time dla learning, not squeezing into overloaded schedule

**Coaching i feedback:**
- **Real-time support:** Available when trying new way
- **Constructive feedback:** "Here's what went well, here's how to improve"
- **Troubleshooting:** Help diagnosing i fixing mistakes

**Remove barriers:**
- **Fix tool issues:** If system is buggy, fix it
- **Adjust workload:** Temporarily reduce other demands
- **Provide resources:** Extra hands, support staff

**Celebrate attempts:**
- **Reward trying:** Even if imperfect
- **Share learning:** "Here's what I learned from my mistake"
- **Patience:** Acknowledge Ability takes time

**Przykład:**
- **Change:** New CRM system
- **Ability building:**
  - First 2 weeks: Reduced sales targets (time dla learning curve)
  - Daily stand-ups: Share struggles i solutions
  - Manager observes calls, gives feedback
  - Month 1: "Mistakes are expected, we're learning together"
  - Month 2: Performance back to normal expectations

**Assessment:** "On scale 1-5, jak dobrze actually używasz new system w daily work?" (If <3, need Ability support)

**Timeline:** Ability takes **LONGEST** - często weeks to months dla proficiency.

### R - Reinforcement (Wzmocnienie)

**Pytanie:** Jak utrzymuję zmianę i nie wracam do starych nawyków?

**Definicja:** Actions i events that sustain change - making new way "stick" i preventing regression.

**Dlaczego ostatnie:** Dopiero teraz (po Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability) możesz reinforce. Can't reinforce coś czego people jeszcze nie robią.

#### Dlaczego Reinforcement fails:

**Regression do old habits:**
- **Old way is easier/faster** (initially)
- **Pressure dla results** pushes back to familiar
- **Lack of consequences:** No one checks jeśli using new way

**Lack of recognition:**
- **New behavior not rewarded**
- **Old behavior still tolerated**
- **Successes not celebrated**

**Changing conditions:**
- **Leadership changes:** New leader doesn't care
- **Next change initiative:** Attention moves elsewhere
- **Crisis:** "No time dla new way, just get it done"

#### Jak dostarczyć Reinforcement:

**Recognition i rewards:**
- **Celebrate successes:** Publicly recognize adoption
- **Tie to performance:** Bonuses, promotions based on new behaviors
- **Gamification:** Leaderboards, badges dla adoption

**Measurement i accountability:**
- **Track adoption:** Metrics showing who uses new way
- **Regular check-ins:** Managers discuss w 1-on-1s
- **Consequences dla non-adoption:** Clear, enforced

**Continuous improvement:**
- **Feedback loops:** "How can we make new way better?"
- **Address ongoing issues:** Don't ignore problems
- **Evolve:** Adapt new way based on learning

**Leadership commitment:**
- **Leaders model behavior:** Don't let leaders use old way
- **Consistent messaging:** Keep talking about change long after launch

**Przykład:**
- **Change:** New CRM system
- **Reinforcement:**
  - Monthly: Report showing CRM usage by person (transparency)
  - Quarterly awards: "CRM Champion" recognition
  - Performance reviews: Include "CRM proficiency" metric
  - Year 1: Regular all-hands showing wins enabled przez CRM
  - Leadership: No exceptions - executives use CRM too

**Assessment:** "On scale 1-5, jak likely jesteś do continue using new system 6 months from now?" (If <3, need more Reinforcement)

**Timeline:** Reinforcement needs to continue **months to years** - until new way becomes "how we do things."

## ADKAR jako Diagnostic Tool

**Power of ADKAR:** Quickly identify WHERE individual jest stuck.

### Diagnostic questions:

**Jeśli ktoś resists change, ask:**

1. **Awareness:** "Do you understand WHY we're changing?"
   - No → Need Awareness work

2. **Desire:** "Do you support this change?"
   - No → Need Desire work (assuming Awareness = Yes)

3. **Knowledge:** "Do you know HOW to change?"
   - No → Need Knowledge/training (assuming Awareness + Desire = Yes)

4. **Ability:** "Can you actually DO it?"
   - No → Need Ability support (assuming Knowledge = Yes)

5. **Reinforcement:** "Are you continuing to use new way?"
   - No → Need Reinforcement (assuming Ability = Yes)

**ADKAR Barrier Point:** First stage gdzie odpowiedź jest "No" - that's gdzie focus intervention.

**Przykład:**
- **Person resisting CRM:**
  - Awareness: "Yes, I know we need better customer tracking" ✓
  - Desire: "But I don't want to change, my Excel works fine" ✗
  - **Barrier:** Desire
  - **Intervention:** Address WIIFM, show benefits, involve w design, incentivize

## ADKAR Scores

**Prosci recommendation:** Score each element 1-5 dla individuals lub groups.

**Individual ADKAR Profile:**
- Awareness: 5 (fully understands why)
- Desire: 2 (low motivation)
- Knowledge: 3 (some training received)
- Ability: 1 (hasn't practiced yet)
- Reinforcement: N/A (not there yet)

**Action:** Focus na Desire (barrier point = 2)

**Group Analysis:**
- Można survey teams, identify common barrier points
- Target interventions based on data

## ADKAR w różnych fazach projektu

**Planning phase:**
- **Design interventions** dla każdego ADKAR element
- Plan communication (Awareness), sponsorship (Desire), training (Knowledge), support (Ability), metrics (Reinforcement)

**Implementation phase:**
- **Execute ADKAR plan**
- Monitor adoption, identify barriers

**Post-implementation:**
- **Reinforce** i sustain
- Prevent regression

## ADKAR + Organizational Change Models

**ADKAR complements organizational models:**

**Kotter 8-Step (organizational) + ADKAR (individual):**
- Kotter Step 1-4 → Build ADKAR Awareness i Desire
- Kotter Step 5 → Enable ADKAR Knowledge i Ability
- Kotter Step 6-8 → Reinforce ADKAR Reinforcement

**Together:** Organizational strategy (Kotter) + Individual adoption (ADKAR) = Complete change approach

## Mistakes w stosowaniu ADKAR

### Mistake 1: Skipping Awareness i Desire, jumping do Training (Knowledge)
- Organizations love training programs
- But training doesn't help jeśli people don't understand why lub don't want to

### Mistake 2: Assuming Awareness = Desire
- "We told them why, so they should want it"
- Understanding ≠ Motivation

### Mistake 3: Confusing Knowledge z Ability
- "We trained them, why aren't they using it?"
- Training ≠ Proficiency (need practice, support)

### Mistake 4: No Reinforcement
- "We launched, we're done"
- Regression happens bez ongoing reinforcement
