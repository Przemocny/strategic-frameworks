# BATNA Framework

## Czym jest?

BATNA (Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement) to concept stworzony przez Rogera Fishera i Williama Ury jako część Principled Negotiation methodology (Harvard Negotiation Project, 1981). BATNA definiuje Twoją **najlepszą alternatywę** jeśli obecna negocjacja się nie uda - innymi słowy, Twój "plan B" lub "walk-away option".

BATNA jest fundamentem negotiation power. Nie chodzi o kto jest bardziej agresywny, kto ma wyższą pozycję, czy kto lepiej bluffuje - **power w negocjacji pochodzi z posiadania strong alternative**. Jeśli masz excellent BATNA, możesz negocjować z pozycji siły. Jeśli masz weak lub no BATNA, jesteś w vulnerable position.

Framework służy do trzech celów: (1) **Identyfikacji** Twojego BATNA, (2) **Improvement** Twojego BATNA before i during negotiation, (3) **Assessment** their BATNA aby understand relative power.

## Dla kogo jest?

- **Negotiators** - każdy kto negocjuje anything (business deals, salary, purchases)
- **Sales professionals** - understanding buyer's alternatives
- **Procurement teams** - developing supplier alternatives
- **Job seekers** - negotiating offers z pozycji siły
- **Entrepreneurs** - fundraising, partnerships, M&A negotiations

## Co można z tego uzyskać?

1. **Clarity na walk-away point** - wiesz kiedy powiedzieć "no"
2. **Confidence w negotiation** - strong BATNA = pewność siebie
3. **Better outcomes** - nie akceptujesz poor deals z desperacji
4. **Realistic expectations** - rozumiesz swoją true leverage
5. **Strategic preparation** - focus energy na improving alternatives

## Dlaczego warto z tego skorzystać?

- **Fundamentalny concept** - core element każdej negotiation training
- **Measurable power** - konkretna, quantifiable alternatywa nie vague "leverage"
- **Protective** - prevents accepting deals worse than your alternatives
- **Proactive** - nie tylko assess, ale actively improve alternatives
- **Universal** - applies do każdej negotiation (business, personal, diplomatic)

## Core Concepts

### BATNA vs Alternatives vs Reservation Point

**Alternatives:** Wszystkie możliwe options jeśli deal fails
**BATNA:** BEST spośród tych alternatives
**Reservation Point (RP):** Worst acceptable deal - threshold poniżej którego wybierasz BATNA

**Example (buying car):**
- **Alternatives:** Buy different car at other dealership, keep current car, use public transport, lease
- **BATNA:** Best alternative = buy similar car at Dealership B dla $25,000
- **Reservation Point:** Won't pay more than $25,000 (because BATNA is $25K)

**Formula:**
```
If Deal Value > BATNA → Accept
If Deal Value < BATNA → Walk away (choose BATNA)
If Deal Value = BATNA → Indifferent
```

### Strong BATNA vs Weak BATNA

**Strong BATNA = Negotiation Power**

**Scenario A (Strong BATNA):**
- Negotiating job offer at Company A
- You have offer from Company B: $120K, great role
- Your BATNA = $120K job at Company B
- **Power:** High - możesz walk if Company A doesn't beat lub match

**Scenario B (Weak BATNA):**
- Negotiating job offer at Company A
- You have no other offers, unemployed 6 months
- Your BATNA = Pozostać unemployed (very unattractive)
- **Power:** Low - prawdopodobnie przyjmiesz almost any reasonable offer

**Key Insight:** W Scenario B nie możesz "create" power przez pretending. Ale możesz **improve BATNA** przez getting more interviews, building freelance pipeline, etc.

## Developing Your BATNA: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: List All Alternatives

**Pytanie:** "Co zrobię jeśli ta negotiation całkowicie się nie uda?"

**Brainstorm wszystkie opcje:**
- Don't self-censor - list everything
- Include "do nothing" jako option
- Consider creative alternatives

**Example (negotiating salary at current job):**
- Alternative 1: Stay at current salary
- Alternative 2: Look for job elsewhere
- Alternative 3: Ask for non-monetary benefits (more vacation, flexibility)
- Alternative 4: Start side business
- Alternative 5: Go back to school, switch careers
- Alternative 6: Relocate to lower cost-of-living area

### Step 2: Evaluate Each Alternative

**Kryteria evaluation:**
- **Feasibility:** Jak realistic jest ta option?
- **Value:** Ile jest worth dla Ciebie? (financial + non-financial)
- **Certainty:** Jak pewna jest ta option?
- **Timeframe:** Jak długo zajmie execution?

**Example evaluation:**

| Alternative | Feasibility | Value | Certainty | Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stay current salary | High | Low ($80K) | High | Immediate |
| New job | Medium | High ($95K+) | Medium | 3-6 months |
| Non-monetary benefits | Medium | Medium (~$85K equivalent) | Medium | Immediate |
| Side business | Medium | Unknown | Low | 6-12 months |
| Back to school | Low | Long-term high | Low | 2+ years |

### Step 3: Select BEST Alternative

**Your BATNA:** The highest-value, most feasible alternative.

**Example:** New job at $95K (if you can secure offer w reasonable timeframe)

**But:** If you DON'T have offer yet, BATNA jest currently "stay at $80K" - to jest important distinction!

### Step 4: Calculate Reservation Point

**Reservation Point:** Threshold gdzie you're indifferent między deal i BATNA.

**Example:**
- BATNA: New job at $95K
- Reservation Point at current job: ~$95K (plus value of staying - familiarity, no job search hassle)
- **Adjusted RP:** Might be $92K (accounting dla hassle of switching)

**You will:**
- **Accept** any offer > $92K
- **Reject** any offer < $92K
- **Negotiate hard** w range $92-100K+

### Step 5: Improve Your BATNA

**Kluczowa strategia:** Don't just identify BATNA - actively **develop better alternatives**.

**How to improve:**

**If BATNA is weak:**
- **Create more alternatives:** Apply to more jobs, develop more vendor options
- **Improve existing alternatives:** Negotiate better terms z Alternative B while negotiating z A
- **Invest w yourself:** Skills, network, credentials that open more doors

**Example (job negotiation):**
- **Week 1:** BATNA = no offer (weak)
- **Action:** Apply aggressively, network, activate recruiters
- **Week 4:** BATNA = $90K offer from Company B (improved!)
- **Action:** Continue interviewing
- **Week 6:** BATNA = $100K offer from Company C (strong!)
- **Result:** Negotiating power dramatically increased

**This is why "always be interviewing" w tech - maintains strong BATNA.**

## Assessing Their BATNA

**Equally important:** Understand other side's BATNA.

**Their BATNA determines THEIR walk-away point.**

### How to assess their BATNA:

**Research:**
- What are their alternatives?
- How attractive are those alternatives?
- What's their urgency/timeline?

**Ask (carefully):**
- "What's your timeline for decision?"
- "Are you considering other options?"
- "What happens if we can't reach agreement?"

**Look for signals:**
- **Weak BATNA signals:** Desperation, pressure, willingness to make concessions quickly
- **Strong BATNA signals:** Patience, walking away easily, mentioning alternatives

**Example (selling to client):**

**If their BATNA is weak:**
- Few alternative vendors
- Urgent deadline
- Switching costs high
→ **You have leverage** - can hold firmer on price/terms

**If their BATNA is strong:**
- Multiple qualified vendors
- No time pressure
- Easy to switch
→ **You have less leverage** - need to differentiate, be flexible

## ZOPA: Zone of Possible Agreement

**ZOPA** = Range gdzie deal is possible = overlap między your RP i their RP.

**Formula:**
```
Your RP (seller): $100K (won't sell below)
Their RP (buyer): $120K (won't pay above)
ZOPA: $100K - $120K

Agreement możliwy anywhere w tym range.
```

**No ZOPA example:**
```
Your RP (seller): $100K (won't sell below)
Their RP (buyer): $80K (won't pay above)
ZOPA: NONE

No agreement possible unless someone improves BATNA lub changes RP.
```

**Visual:**
```
You (Seller)                    They (Buyer)
BATNA: $95K                     BATNA: Better deal at $80K
RP: $100K ←------ZOPA------→ RP: $120K
        $100K         $120K

Agreement possible w range $100-120K
Exact outcome depends na negotiation skill, tactics, information asymmetry
```

## Using BATNA w Negotiation

### DO: Use BATNA as Shield

**Protect yourself od bad deals:**
- Internally: "My RP is $X based on BATNA. I will walk if below."
- Gives confidence to say "no"

**Example:**
- Offer: $85K
- Your BATNA: $90K job elsewhere
- **Response:** "I appreciate the offer, but I have alternatives that are more attractive financially. Can you improve?"

### DO: Improve BATNA Before i During

**Timing matters:**
- **Before negotiation:** Develop alternatives to enter z strong position
- **During negotiation:** Continue developing alternatives (don't put all eggs w one basket)

**Example (vendor negotiation):**
- **Before:** Get quotes from 3 vendors
- **During:** While negotiating z Vendor A, continue discussions z B i C
- **Result:** If A knows you have ready alternatives, more likely to compete on price/terms

### DON'T: Reveal Weak BATNA

**If your BATNA is poor:**
- **Don't say:** "I'm desperate, I'll take anything"
- **Do say:** Focus na value you bring, objective criteria

**Example (salary negotiation when unemployed):**
- **Wrong:** "I really need this job, I've been unemployed for months"
- **Right:** "Based on market rates dla my experience i the value I'll bring, I'm looking for $X-Y range"

### DON'T: Bluff About BATNA

**Lying destroys credibility i relationships.**

**Wrong:**
- "I have offer dla $150K" (gdy nie masz)
- Risk: They call bluff, you lose credibility + deal

**Right:**
- If no BATNA: Focus na other aspects (value, objective criteria, creative options)
- Or: Work on developing real BATNA before negotiating

### DO: Make Their Decision Easy (Weaken Their BATNA Perception)

**Show why your offer is better than their alternatives:**
- "Compared to Alternative X, our solution offers Y benefits"
- Highlight switching costs, risks, delays w their BATNA
- Make your offer the clear best choice

## BATNA w Different Contexts

### Salary Negotiation

**Your BATNA:**
- Other job offers
- Current job (if employed)
- Freelancing/consulting
- Waiting dla better opportunity (if you can afford)

**Improving BATNA:**
- Interview at multiple companies simultaneously
- Build freelance clients jako backup
- Upskill to open more opportunities

**Example:**
- Weak BATNA: Unemployed, no offers → Accept reasonable offer
- Strong BATNA: 3 offers ranging $100-120K → Negotiate aggressively for $130K+

### Vendor/Supplier Negotiation

**Your BATNA (as buyer):**
- Alternative vendors
- In-house solution
- Do without (if not critical)

**Improving BATNA:**
- Develop multiple qualified vendors
- Build internal capability
- Reduce dependency

**Example:**
- Single vendor (weak BATNA): They have pricing power
- 3 qualified vendors (strong BATNA): You can play them against each other

### Fundraising (Startup)

**Your BATNA:**
- Other investor offers
- Bootstrapping
- Revenue-based financing
- Strategic partnerships

**Improving BATNA:**
- Talk to many investors
- Grow revenue (reduces dependency na VC)
- Create competitive dynamic

**Example:**
- One term sheet (weak): Accept unfavorable terms
- Multiple term sheets (strong): Negotiate dla best valuation i terms

### Real Estate (Buying)

**Your BATNA:**
- Other houses on market
- Rent longer
- Buy w different area
- Wait dla better market

**Improving BATNA:**
- View many houses, be ready to move on
- Be pre-approved (ready to buy elsewhere)
- Not emotionally attached to one house

**Example:**
- "This is THE house" (no BATNA): Seller has power, you overpay
- "Nice house, but I have 3 others I like" (strong BATNA): Negotiating power

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: No BATNA Development

**Problem:** Entering negotiation bez alternatives
**Fix:** Always develop Plan B, C, D before negotiating

### Mistake 2: Falling in Love (Weak BATNA Thinking)

**Problem:** Emotional attachment kills BATNA ("I MUST have this")
**Fix:** Always maintain alternatives, emotional distance

### Mistake 3: Revealing BATNA Too Early

**Problem:** Showing your cards limits upside
**Fix:** Keep BATNA as backstop, negotiate dla better than BATNA

### Mistake 4: Static BATNA (Not Improving)

**Problem:** Identifying BATNA once i stopping
**Fix:** Continuously improve alternatives throughout negotiation

### Mistake 5: Ignoring Their BATNA

**Problem:** Only thinking about your side
**Fix:** Research i assess their alternatives

## BATNA + Principled Negotiation

**BATNA integrates z Principled Negotiation:**

1. **Know your BATNA** (negotiation power)
2. **Separate people from problem** (relationship)
3. **Focus on interests** (expand pie)
4. **Invent options** (beat both BATNAs)
5. **Use objective criteria** (fairness)

**Goal:** Reach agreement **better than both parties' BATNAs**. If can't, walk away.

## Advanced: Revealing BATNA Strategically

**Sometimes revealing BATNA helps:**

**When to reveal:**
- BATNA is genuinely strong i verifiable
- Creates urgency dla other side
- Competition motivates better offer

**How to reveal:**
- Factually, not as threat
- "I want to be transparent - I have offer from X at $Y terms. I prefer working with you, but need comparable terms."

**Example (job offer):**
- "I've received offer from [Company] at $120K. I'm very interested w your company i culture, but need to make financially sound decision. Can you match lub exceed?"

**Risk:** They might walk away. Only do this if willing to lose deal.

## Kluczowe osoby

### Roger Fisher & William Ury
Creators of BATNA concept jako część Getting to Yes framework (Harvard Negotiation Project, 1981). BATNA jest arguably most impactful contribution - changed how people think about negotiation power.

### Thomas Schelling
Nobel Prize economist, "The Strategy of Conflict" (1960) - laid theoretical groundwork dla commitment i alternatives w negotiation (pre-dated BATNA term).
