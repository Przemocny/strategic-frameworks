# Lewin's Change Model (Unfreeze-Change-Refreeze)

## Czym jest?

Lewin's Change Model, znany też jako **Unfreeze-Change-Refreeze**, to pionierski framework zarządzania zmianą organizacyjną stworzony przez Kurta Lewina, psychologa społecznego i pioniera organizational development, w latach 40. XX wieku. Model opisuje change jako trzyfazowy proces: **Unfreeze** (rozmrożenie status quo), **Change** (transition do nowego stanu), **Refreeze** (zamrożenie nowego stanu jako new normal).

Framework bazuje na metaforze lodu: aby zmienić kształt bloku lodu, musisz najpierw go rozmrozić (unfreeze), zmienić kształt wody (change), a następnie zamrozić w nowym kształcie (refreeze). Jest to jeden z **najstarszych i najczęściej cytowanych** change models, foundation dla późniejszych frameworks (Kotter, ADKAR, etc.).

Mimo że powstał 80 lat temu, pozostaje relevant bo captures fundamental truth: **change requires disrupting equilibrium, transitioning, then stabilizing new state**.

## Dla kogo jest?

- **Change leaders** - rozumiejących fundamentals of change process
- **Managers** - prowadzących teams przez transitions
- **Organizational development professionals** - designing change initiatives
- **Studenci zarządzania** - learning foundational theory
- **Anyone leading change** - prosty, intuitive framework

## Co można z tego uzyskać?

1. **Conceptual clarity** - simple 3-stage model łatwy do zrozumienia i wyjaśnienia
2. **Focus na preparation** - emphasizes importance of "unfreezing" before rushing to change
3. **Stabilization awareness** - reminds że change musi być "refrozen" lub regresses
4. **Foundation thinking** - base dla zrozumienia bardziej complex models
5. **Field theory application** - rozumienie forces driving i restraining change

## Dlaczego warto z tego skorzystać?

- **Foundational framework** - każdy subsequent model builds on lub reacts do Lewin
- **Simple i memorable** - 3 stages, intuitive metaphor
- **Grounded w psychology** - based on social psychology research
- **Timeless principles** - 80 lat później, still relevant
- **Complementary** - works alongside other models (provides high-level structure)

## Trzy Fazy Modelu Lewina

### Phase 1: Unfreeze (Rozmrożenie)

**Cel:** Prepare organization dla change - break down existing status quo before building up new way.

**Metafora:** Rozmrozić lód zanim możesz nadać mu nowy kształt.

**Dlaczego Unfreeze jest kluczowe:**
- **Equilibrium:** Organizacje naturally seek stability - people comfortable z current state
- **Resistance:** Change disrupts comfort - natural human reaction to resist
- **Without Unfreeze:** Próba change bez preparation meets massive resistance i fails

#### Kluczowe koncepty Unfreeze:

**Force Field Analysis (Lewin's tool):**

Każda sytuacja ma **driving forces** (push for change) i **restraining forces** (resist change).

**Current State = Equilibrium** między driving i restraining forces.

**To Unfreeze:**
- **Increase driving forces:** Make case dla change stronger
- **Decrease restraining forces:** Address barriers, fears, obstacles
- **Lub both**

**Diagram:**
```
Driving Forces →        ← Restraining Forces
(Change)                (Status Quo)
                  |
         Current State
           (Frozen)
```

**Example (implementing new software):**

**Driving Forces:**
- Old system crashes frequently
- Competitors using better technology
- Leadership mandate
- Cost savings potential

**Restraining Forces:**
- Fear of learning new system
- "Old way works fine dla me"
- Time investment required
- Uncertainty about job security

**To Unfreeze:**
- Increase driving forces: Show data on lost productivity from crashes, competitive threat
- Decrease restraining forces: Address fears (training, job security), involve people w selection

#### Activities w Unfreeze phase:

**Create awareness of need:**
- **Communicate why change is necessary**
- Share data, trends, customer feedback showing problem
- Create sense of urgency (similar do Kotter Step 1)

**Build desire dla change:**
- **Address "What's in it dla me?"**
- Show benefits, not just organizational need
- Involve stakeholders w planning

**Challenge status quo:**
- **Question assumptions:** "Why do we do it this way?"
- Disrupt complacency
- Create discomfort z current state

**Reduce resistance:**
- **Listen to concerns** i address fears
- Provide information to reduce uncertainty
- Build trust through transparency

**Przykład:**
- **Company transitioning do remote work:**
  - Unfreeze: Town halls discussing future of work trends, employee surveys showing desire dla flexibility, leadership openly questioning "Do we need office?"
  - Address fears: "Your job is secure, we're expanding talent pool"
  - Build case: Cost savings, talent retention data

**Common mistakes w Unfreeze:**
- **Rushing:** Skipping straight do Change bez proper Unfreeze = resistance
- **Under-communicating:** People don't understand why change is needed
- **Ignoring emotions:** Focusing tylko na logic, ignoring fear/anxiety

**Timeline:** Unfreeze może trwać **weeks to months** - depends na scale of change i organizational readiness.

### Phase 2: Change (Transition / Movement)

**Cel:** Execute transition z old state do new state - gdzie actual change happens.

**Metafora:** Zmiana kształtu wody - fluid, uncertain, uncomfortable period.

**Charakterystyka Change phase:**
- **Uncertainty:** People nie są jeszcze comfortable z new way
- **Learning curve:** Developing new skills, behaviors, mindsets
- **Productivity dip:** Performance often drops initially (J-curve)
- **Emotional:** Confusion, frustration, anxiety, excitement

#### Co się dzieje w Change phase:

**Learning i development:**
- **Training programs:** Teaching new skills
- **Coaching:** Supporting individuals
- **Resources:** Providing tools, information

**New structures i processes:**
- **Implement new systems:** Roll out technology, processes
- **Reorganization:** If needed, restructure teams/roles
- **Pilot programs:** Test w small groups before full rollout

**Communication intensifies:**
- **Frequent updates:** "Here's where we are, what's next"
- **Two-way dialogue:** Listen to feedback, adjust
- **Celebrate small wins:** Maintain momentum

**Support mechanisms:**
- **Change champions:** Advocates helping peers
- **Help desks:** Support when stuck
- **Manager coaching:** 1-on-1 assistance

#### The Transition Curve (often overlaid na Lewin):

People go through emotional journey during Change:

1. **Denial:** "This won't affect me"
2. **Resistance/Anger:** "I don't like this"
3. **Exploration:** "Maybe this could work"
4. **Commitment:** "I'm on board"

**Leadership role:** Help people move through curve faster.

**Przykład:**
- **Company transitioning do remote work:**
  - Change phase:
    - Week 1-2: Set up home offices, train on collaboration tools (Zoom, Slack)
    - Month 1-3: Adjust workflows, experiment z async communication
    - Ongoing: Regular check-ins, share best practices, iterate on processes
    - Support: IT help desk, manager training on remote leadership

**Common mistakes w Change:**
- **Inadequate support:** Expecting people to figure it out alone
- **Moving too fast:** Not allowing time dla learning
- **Lack of communication:** Leaving people w dark about progress
- **Ignoring feedback:** Not adapting based on what's learned

**Timeline:** Change phase typically **longest** - months to year+ depending na complexity.

### Phase 3: Refreeze (Zamrożenie / Stabilizacja)

**Cel:** Stabilize organization w new state - make change "stick" i become new normal.

**Metafora:** Zamrożenie wody w nowym kształcie - solidify change.

**Dlaczego Refreeze jest kluczowe:**
- **Without Refreeze:** Risk of regression do old ways
- **Embed change:** Make new behaviors habitual, automatic
- **New equilibrium:** Establish stability after turbulent Change phase

#### Activities w Refreeze phase:

**Institutionalize new ways:**
- **Update policies i procedures:** Formal documentation of new processes
- **Align systems:** HR systems (performance reviews, compensation) reflect new behaviors
- **Standard operating procedures:** New way becomes "how we do things"

**Reinforce behaviors:**
- **Recognition i rewards:** Celebrate adoption, reward desired behaviors
- **Consequences dla old behaviors:** Make clear old way jest no longer acceptable
- **Leadership modeling:** Leaders consistently demonstrate new way

**Measure i monitor:**
- **KPIs dla new state:** Track metrics showing adoption
- **Regular reviews:** Check że people aren't slipping back
- **Continuous improvement:** Refine new way based on experience

**Celebrate success:**
- **Acknowledge journey:** Recognize teams i individuals who made it happen
- **Share wins:** Communicate results, benefits realized
- **Cement cultural shift:** Stories, heroes, rituals around new way

**Przykład:**
- **Company transitioning do remote work:**
  - Refreeze phase:
    - Update employee handbook: Remote work jako standard option
    - Performance reviews: Focus na outcomes nie hours in office
    - Onboarding: New hires start remote by default
    - Company culture: "We're a remote-first company" becomes identity
    - Office space: Downsize physical office (commitment signal)

**Common mistakes w Refreeze:**
- **Declaring victory too early:** Change not fully embedded, regression occurs
- **Not updating systems:** Old systems still reward old behaviors
- **Moving to next change immediately:** Change fatigue, nothing ever stabilizes
- **Lack of leadership commitment:** Leaders stop modeling, signal change wasn't serious

**Timeline:** Refreeze requires **months to years** - change becomes culture, which is slow.

## Krytyka i ewolucja Lewin's Model

### Krytyka "Refreeze":

**Argument:** W dzisiejszym fast-paced, constantly changing environment, **"Refreeze" jest unrealistic** - organizacje muszą być continuously adapting.

**Response:** "Refreeze" nie znaczy "frozen forever" - znaczy **stabilize enough** aby nowy state stał się foundation dla next change. Without some stability, constant chaos.

**Modern interpretation:** "Refreeze" = **Consolidate** lub **Stabilize** - establish new normal until next evolution.

### Rozszerzenia Lewin's Model:

**Kotter 8-Step:**
- Unfreeze = Steps 1-4 (Urgency, Coalition, Vision, Communication)
- Change = Steps 5-6 (Empower, Wins)
- Refreeze = Steps 7-8 (Consolidate, Anchor)

**ADKAR:**
- Unfreeze = Awareness, Desire
- Change = Knowledge, Ability
- Refreeze = Reinforcement

**Lewin provides high-level structure, later models provide operational detail.**

## Force Field Analysis: Lewin's Diagnostic Tool

**Użycie:** Analyze situation przed attempting change.

### Jak używać:

**Step 1: Define desired change**
- Current state: X
- Desired state: Y

**Step 2: List Driving Forces (pro-change)**
- External pressures
- Internal motivations
- Benefits of change

**Step 3: List Restraining Forces (anti-change)**
- Barriers
- Resistance
- Costs of change

**Step 4: Assess strength każdej force (1-5)**

**Step 5: Strategize**
- Które driving forces możesz strengthen?
- Które restraining forces możesz weaken?

**Example (Moving to cloud infrastructure):**

**Driving Forces (Score 1-5):**
- Scalability needs (5)
- Cost reduction potential (4)
- Competitor pressure (3)
- Innovation opportunity (3)
**Total: 15**

**Restraining Forces (Score 1-5):**
- Security concerns (5)
- Learning curve (4)
- Migration complexity (4)
- Loss of control perception (2)
**Total: 15**

**Current State: Equilibrium (15 = 15)**

**To Unfreeze i enable Change:**
- Strengthen driving force: Demonstrate cost savings z POC (4→5)
- Weaken restraining force: Address security (hire expert, certifications) (5→2)
- **New balance:** Driving 16 > Restraining 12 → Change possible

## Lewin w praktyce: Full Example

**Scenario:** Manufacturing company modernizing quality control process.

### Phase 1: Unfreeze (2 months)

**Activities:**
- **Create awareness:** Share data on defect rates, customer complaints, competitor quality
- **Town halls:** CEO explains why current quality process insufficient dla market demands
- **Force Field Analysis:** Workshop z teams identifying driving/restraining forces
- **Address fears:** "No layoffs - we're adding capability, not replacing people"
- **Involve:** Quality team participates w selecting new system

**Outcome:** Broad understanding że change jest necessary, reduced resistance.

### Phase 2: Change (6 months)

**Activities:**
- **Training:** 3-week program on new quality methods (Six Sigma)
- **Pilot:** Test new process w one production line
- **Support:** Quality coaches available on floor
- **Iterate:** Adjust process based on frontline feedback
- **Communicate:** Weekly updates on progress, wins, challenges

**Challenges:**
- Initial productivity drop (learning curve)
- Some frustration z new procedures
- Technical issues z new inspection equipment

**Outcome:** New quality process operational, teams building proficiency.

### Phase 3: Refreeze (12 months)

**Activities:**
- **Expand:** Roll out do all production lines
- **Update SOPs:** New quality process documented formally
- **Performance metrics:** Quality KPIs w employee reviews
- **Celebrate:** Defect rate down 40%, customer satisfaction up - company-wide recognition
- **Certifications:** Invest w Six Sigma training dla all quality staff
- **Culture:** "Quality first" becomes company value, reinforced constantly

**Outcome:** New quality process jest "how we do things" - embedded w culture.

**3 years later:** When next process improvement needed, foundation jest solid dla building next change.

## Lewin vs Modern Continuous Change

**Debate:** Lewin's linear 3-stage vs Continuous Change mindset.

**Lewin's view:** Change jako discrete events - periods of stability interrupted by change
**Modern view:** Change jako constant - organizations always evolving

**Reality:** **Both are true depending na context**
- **Transformational changes:** Benefit from Lewin structure (Unfreeze-Change-Refreeze)
- **Incremental improvements:** Continuous improvement mindset (Agile, Kaizen)

**Hybrid approach:** Use Lewin dla major transformations, continuous improvement dla ongoing optimization.

## Kluczowe osoby

### Kurt Lewin (1890-1947)
German-American psychologist, pioneer w social psychology, organizational development, i applied research. Famous dla quote: **"There is nothing so practical as a good theory."** Developed Field Theory, Group Dynamics, Action Research - wszystkie fundamenty modern OD. Change Model (1940s) był groundbreaking i remains influential.

### Edgar Schein
MIT professor, student of Lewin's ideas, expanded organizational culture theory. Connected Lewin's work do modern organizational development.

- **Organizational Development departments:** Foundation dla change initiatives
